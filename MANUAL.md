# Pitstop — Team Manual

*A guide for the team on how to use Pitstop to plan, track, and deliver work.*

---

## Why Pitstop exists

No tool will replace work. Pitstop exists to give the team **clarity and shared visibility** — not to generate reports or bureaucracy. The goal is that every team member knows what they are doing, why it matters, and what comes next.

A few principles to keep in mind:
- Over-planning does damage too. Use the tool lightly.
- History of how we have done something is crucial — record it here, not in memory.
- Pacing and rhythm matter. Speed is cadence, not sprint.
- Maps give context and focus. Decisions are not to escalate — they are to record.

---

## The hierarchy

Everything in Pitstop follows a five-level structure:

```
Program
  └── Goals
        └── Pitstops
              └── Checklists
                    ← Activities run alongside all of this
```

**Program** — A funding stream, geographic programme, or strategic initiative. Groups related goals together. Example: *North Zone Livelihoods Programme*.

**Goal** — An end in itself. A goal should be meaningful on its own, not just a step toward something else. It has an owner, a target date, and a status. Example: *Enroll 200 children in after-school centres by June*.

**Pitstop** — A means to an end, and a breaker of large work into trackable pieces. Each pitstop is a milestone or deliverable within a goal. It is not a task — it is a meaningful checkpoint. Example: *Baseline assessment of 5 clusters complete*.

**Checklist** — Self-assessed milestones within a pitstop. Think of these as your SOP — the practical steps you know need to happen to call the pitstop done. Example: *Visit cluster / Meet coordinator / Submit report*.

**Activity** — A scheduled meeting, field visit, or event linked to one or more pitstops. Activities have attendees, a date and time, and a location. They appear on the team calendar.

## Getting started

### 1. Create a Goal

Go to **Goals** (`/dashboard`). Click **New Goal**.

- Write a title that is an outcome, not an action. *"Creche enrolment reaches 150"* is better than *"Do creche work"*.
- Assign an owner — the person accountable, not necessarily doing all the work.
- Set a target date.
- Add a description if the goal needs context for the rest of the team.
- Tag it to a **Program**, **Zone**, and **Cluster** if relevant. This drives the map and geo-filtered views.

### 2. Break it into Pitstops

Inside the goal, click **Add Pitstop**.

- Each pitstop should represent a real deliverable — something you can point to and say "this is done."
- Set a **start date** and **target date**. These appear on the Gantt chart and timeline.
- Assign an **owner**. If different from the goal owner, the pitstop owner is responsible for this specific piece.
- Choose a **type**: Meeting, Workshop, Visit, Training, Report, Review, or Other.
- Set a **priority**: High, Medium, or Low.

Good pitstop sequencing matters. Arrange pitstops in the order work should flow — the route map on the goal page shows this visually.

### 3. Add a Checklist

Inside each pitstop, add checklist items for the practical steps. These are your SOP. They should be specific enough that someone else could follow them.

Checklist progress is shown on the goal route map and feeds into the AI assistant's analysis.

### 4. Schedule Activities

When a pitstop requires a field visit, meeting, or team event, create an **Activity** (`/activities`).

- Link it to the relevant pitstop(s).
- Add attendees from the team.
- Set a location.

Activities sync to Outlook and Google Calendar — use the **Subscribe** button on the Activities page to get your personal calendar feed.

## Day-to-day use

### Field Notes (Standup)

Go to **Field Notes** (`/standup`) to log your daily update:
- What did you do yesterday?
- What are you doing today?
- Any blockers?

This is not a report. It is a signal to the rest of the team. The AI assistant reads these to give you a current picture of team momentum.

### Updating Pitstop status

When you start a pitstop, move it to **In Progress**. When it is done, mark it **Done**. Do not leave pitstops in Upcoming when work has started — the team cannot see your progress otherwise.

Statuses: **Upcoming → In Progress → Done**

Pitstops can also be **Flagged** (blocked or at risk) — use this to surface blockers early.

### Fortnightly Review

Every two weeks, go to **Review** (`/review`) for a structured look at committed vs. delivered. This is the team's shared accountability moment — not a blame exercise, but a signal of what is realistic and what needs attention.

## Planning tools

### Gantt Chart (`/gantt`)
Visual timeline of all goals and pitstops. Use this to spot overloading — if one person has five pitstops due in the same week, the Gantt makes it obvious.

### Timeline (`/timeline`)
A vertical scroll view of pitstops by date. Good for a quick "what's happening this week" view.

### Planner (`/planner`)
Personal weekly planning. Use this to organise your own work for the week ahead.

### Quarters (`/quarters`)
Goals can be assigned to planning quarters. Use this for cycle-based planning.

---

## Making sense of the work

### Themes (`/themes`)
Tag pitstops with cross-cutting themes like *Livelihoods*, *Health*, or *Child Protection*. Themes let you see all work on a topic regardless of which goal or programme it sits in.

### Geography (`/geography` and `/map`)
Tag goals and pitstops to **Zones** (North, South, East, West, Central) and **Clusters** (sub-areas within zones).

The **Programme Map** (`/map`) shows all settlements, resource centres, children's centres, youth centres, and creches as a live map. Click any settlement to see linked goals and pitstops. Click a zone or cluster boundary to see a summary of work in that area.

### Risks (`/risks`)
When you identify a threat to a goal — funding delay, staff change, external dependency — log it as a risk. Give it a likelihood and impact. Don't wait until it happens.

### Decisions (`/decisions`)
Key decisions made in the team should be recorded here, linked to the relevant goal. The purpose is not to escalate — it is to have a record of why we did what we did.

### Metrics (`/goals/[id]`)
Each goal can have quantitative KPIs — for example *Households reached* with a target of 500. Add data points periodically to track progress against the number.

## The AI assistant

Click **Pitstop AI** in the sidebar (desktop) or the ✦ button (mobile).

The assistant has access to all goals, pitstops, checklists, themes, risks, decisions, activities, field notes, metrics, and geography in real time. It can:

- Analyse overall progress and flag what's at risk
- Identify overdue pitstops across the team
- Summarise workload per team member
- Suggest a realistic schedule for the year
- Recommend meetings and field visits based on pitstop status
- Suggest improvements to goal breakdowns

The assistant knows all the features of the app and can direct you to the right module. Ask it anything specific — it will not give generic advice.

## Objective setting (annual cycle)

The whiteboard framework behind this app describes three connected moments:

**Objective Setting** — Goals should link to the plan, be meaningful to the individual, and support self-assessment and shared visibility.

**Annual Plan** — Break down goals, assess load, clarify ownership, and check budget/holistic feasibility.

**Operations & Clarity** — Day-to-day use of the tool to maintain direction, focus, and cadence. New muscles for the team. Not just for senior staff — for every team member to have clarity on what they are doing and why.

The pyramid is: *Programme → Plan → Goals → Pitstops → Checklists*, with Activities running alongside. Start at the top with purpose, work down to action.

| Where you are | What to do |
|---|---|
| New goal | Goals → New Goal → set owner, date, geography, programme |
| New pitstop | Inside goal → Add Pitstop → set dates, owner, type |
| Update progress | Open pitstop → move status → tick checklist items |
| Log a blocker | Set pitstop status to Flagged, add a note |
| Schedule a visit | Activities → New → link to pitstop, add attendees |
| Daily update | Field Notes → log yesterday / today / blockers |
| See the big picture | Gantt or Timeline |
| Something went wrong | Risks → log it |
| A decision was made | Decisions → record it |
| Ask a question | Pitstop AI → ask anything |
