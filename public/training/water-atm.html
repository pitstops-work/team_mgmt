PITSTOP 1 OF 6 · RESEARCH · 21 DAYS

Source Water Assessment & Site Selection

Why this pitstop

The most critical first step — everything depends on source water quality and site viability. RP leads the feasibility assessment: land permission, borewell approval, regulatory clearances, and site suitability. No capital is committed before RP confirms the site.

1.
Collect water sample from proposed borewell or source and send to certified lab
✓Water Sample Collection & Dispatch
2.
Analyse for: TDS, pH, hardness, fluoride, nitrate, iron, arsenic, turbidity, E. coli, chloride
📎Water Quality Lab Analysis Review
3.
Confirm TDS >500 mg/L (NGT prohibits RO on municipal water below this — document result)
✓TDS Compliance Verification
4.
RP conducts feasibility assessment: land permission, borewell permission, regulatory viability
🎙Site Feasibility Assessment Visit
5.
RP applies for / follows up on CGWB or state ground water board borewell approval
✓CGWB Borewell Approval Follow-up
6.
RP follows up on SPCB Consent to Establish and municipal body NoC
✓SPCB & Municipal NoC Follow-up
7.
Identify plant site: minimum 15×12 ft, clean, ventilated, flood-safe, not near drains or toilets
✓Plant Site Identification
8.
Confirm site is within 200 metres walking distance of all target households
✓Site Proximity Verification
9.
Secure land tenure: formal lease, MoU, or NoC from landowner / civic body
✓Land Tenure Documentation
10.
Confirm commercial electrical connection available (or plan solar/UPS backup)
✓Electrical Connection Confirmation
11.
Plan reject water (brine) disposal: municipal drain, toilet flushing, or horticulture — never back into source aquifer
✓Reject Water Disposal Planning
12.
RP reviews water test results and confirms site before any procurement begins
✓Water Test Review & Site Sign-off
PITSTOP 2 OF 6 · RESEARCH · 21 DAYS

Demand Estimation & Plant Sizing

Why this pitstop

Size the plant correctly before procurement. An underpowered plant kills adoption; an overpowered one wastes capital. Target: 250 households → recommended capacity: 500–1,000 LPH. RP reviews and approves specs.

1.
Confirm target household count: 250 HH
✓Household Target Confirmation
2.
Estimate daily demand: 15–20 litres per household per day (drinking + cooking)
✓Daily Demand Estimation
3.
Apply adoption rate buffer: design for 60–70% adoption initially
✓Adoption Rate Buffer Calculation
4.
Apply peak season buffer: add 30–40% for summer months (April–June)
✓Peak Season Capacity Planning
5.
Select plant capacity: 500–1,000 LPH based on 250 HH
✓Plant Capacity Selection
6.
Account for recovery ratio (50–65%): raw water feed must be 1.5–2× product output
✓Recovery Ratio Calculation
7.
Get at least 2 vendor quotes — confirm capex includes tanks, pipework, ATM kiosk, electrical, civil
✓Vendor Quote Collection
8.
Plan reject water volume and confirm disposal route can handle it
✓Reject Water Volume Planning
9.
RP reviews demand estimate and approves plant specifications before procurement
✓Plant Specification Approval
PITSTOP 3 OF 6 · BUDGETING · 45 DAYS

Procurement & Installation

Why this pitstop

Procure and install all plant components. Antiscalant dosing is mandatory for Bangalore's high-silica borewell water — skipping it is the most common cause of premature membrane failure. RP inspects the completed setup before commissioning.

1.
Procure raw water storage tank (5,000–10,000L overhead or underground)
✓Raw Water Tank Procurement
2.
Procure pre-filtration train: pressure sand filter → activated carbon filter → micron cartridge (5–10 micron)
✓Pre-Filtration Train Procurement
3.
Procure antiscalant dosing pump (mandatory for Bangalore high-silica/high-hardness borewell water)
✓Antiscalant Dosing Pump Procurement
4.
Procure high-pressure pump sized for source TDS
✓High-Pressure Pump Procurement
5.
Procure RO membrane array and pressure vessels
✓RO Membrane Array Procurement
6.
Procure UV steriliser (post-RO) and TDS controller/mineral dosing unit
✓UV Steriliser & TDS Unit Procurement
7.
Procure product water storage tank (1,000–5,000L food-grade HDPE or stainless steel)
✓Product Water Tank Procurement
8.
Procure Water ATM kiosk unit with RFID reader, payment interface, and flow meter
✓Water ATM Kiosk Procurement
9.
Set up control panel with automated backwash timer (every 6 hours) and GSM/SIM monitoring
✓Control Panel & GSM Setup
10.
Complete civil work: plant room, pipework, electrical connection
✓Civil Work & Electrical Completion
11.
Complete full installation and test all components end-to-end
✓Full Installation & End-to-End Test
12.
RP inspects completed installation before commissioning
✓Installation Inspection Visit
PITSTOP 4 OF 6 · MEETING · 40 DAYS

Community Engagement & Payment Setup

Why this pitstop

Run parallel to installation. Household registration, RFID card distribution, operator recruitment, and health awareness. Women's SHGs are the most effective adoption channel — their buy-in determines whether households switch from free (contaminated) sources to the ATM.

1.
Register all target households and issue RFID smart cards
✓Household Registration & Card Issuance
2.
Load ₹20–30 trial credit on each card to break first-use barrier
✓Trial Credit Loading on Cards
3.
Set up payment system: RFID cards primary + UPI/GPay secondary
✓Payment System Setup
4.
Recruit plant operator from community (preference: woman from SHG)
✓Plant Operator Recruitment
5.
Train operator: daily startup/shutdown, register maintenance, card recharges, basic troubleshooting
🎙Plant Operator Training
6.
Engage women's SHGs — brief them on water quality and plant model; get their active endorsement
✓SHG Briefing & Endorsement
7.
Conduct health linkage awareness sessions: water quality, disease connection, why RO matters
✓Water Safety Awareness Sessions
8.
Conduct nukkad natak or community meeting on water safety and launch
🎙Community Launch Meeting / Nukkad Natak
9.
Set and communicate pricing: per-litre rate that covers operating costs (reference: ₹4 per 20L)
✓Pricing Communication to Community
10.
RP attends key SHG meeting to support community trust and address concerns
✓SHG Engagement Meeting
PITSTOP 5 OF 6 · SITE VISIT · 50 DAYS

Compliance, Testing & Launch

Why this pitstop

Launch the plant and ATM. RP makes frequent visits during the first few weeks — early adoption, teething issues with equipment, and community hesitation all need hands-on support. Compliance documentation must be in order before water is sold.

1.
Apply for FSSAI registration or licence (mandatory before commercial water supply)
✓FSSAI Registration Application
2.
Conduct post-installation water quality test: TDS 100–200 mg/L, pH 6.5–8.5, zero E. coli
📎Post-Installation Water Quality Test
3.
Document source TDS test result on file (NGT compliance record)
✓NGT Compliance Documentation
4.
Confirm reject water disposal route is functional and not re-entering source aquifer
✓Reject Water Disposal Verification
5.
Confirm ATM kiosk is placed at a high-visibility community node (near temple, school, main lane)
✓ATM Kiosk Placement Verification
6.
Activate plant and ATM — confirm RFID cards, payment system, and flow meter are working
✓Plant & ATM Activation
7.
Communicate launch date to all registered households
✓Launch Date Communication
8.
RP present at launch day
✓Water ATM Launch & Community Rollout
9.
RP makes frequent site visits during first 2–3 weeks: adoption check, equipment issues, community hesitation
🎙Early Adoption Site Visits
10.
RP reviews first-week dispensed volume and household adoption rate
✓First Week Adoption Review
PITSTOP 6 OF 6 · REVIEW · 80 DAYS

Monitoring & Operations

Why this pitstop

Monthly monitoring of 1 Water ATM plant(s) serving ~250 households. RP conducts monthly plant visit and regular dashboard reviews to catch quality decline, revenue leakage, or maintenance failures early. Plant is a standing agenda item in the monthly cluster meeting.

1.
Product TDS checked and logged (target: 100–200 mg/L)
✓Product TDS Check & Log
2.
pH and turbidity checked; monthly microbial (E. coli) lab test done
📎Water Quality & Microbial Test
3.
Daily litres dispensed vs. expected volume reconciled
✓Daily Volume Reconciliation
4.
Revenue vs. flow meter logs cross-checked (divergence = theft or metering fault)
✓Revenue vs Flow Meter Audit
5.
Card recharge frequency per household reviewed (no recharge in 2 weeks = follow up)
✓Household Recharge Usage Review
6.
Membrane flux monitored — >15% drop in permeate flow triggers chemical clean or replacement
✓Membrane Flux Check
7.
Pre-filter cartridge replacement checked (every 1–3 months in turbid borewell water)
✓Pre-Filter Cartridge Check
8.
UV lamp hours tracked (replace annually at ~8,000 hours)
✓UV Lamp Hours Review
9.
Borewell yield and water level logged (Bangalore: aquifers depleting — check quarterly)
✓Borewell Yield & Level Log
10.
Backwash cycle log reviewed — missing cycles indicate control panel fault
✓Backwash Cycle Log Review
11.
Downtime log reviewed (target: >95% uptime; MTTR <4 hours minor, <24 hours major)
✓Plant Downtime Log Review
12.
Antiscalant and consumable stock checked — maintain 1–2 months buffer
✓Consumable Stock Check
13.
RP reviews operational dashboard — TDS trend, litres dispensed, downtime, revenue
✓Operational Dashboard Review
14.
RP monthly visit to plant — reviews all metrics with operator
🎙Monthly Plant Monitoring Visit
15.
Water ATM reviewed as agenda item in monthly cluster meeting
✓Water ATM Cluster Meeting Item
