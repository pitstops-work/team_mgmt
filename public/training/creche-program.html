WHY THIS GOAL MATTERS

The first 1,000 days decide a lifetime

In our slums, working mothers leave 6-month-olds with older siblings — or alone. The creche is the difference between a child who is school-ready at 6 and one who isn't.

6 mo – 6 yrs

the age window we serve. Brain development in these years sets cognitive ceiling for the rest of life — yet this is exactly when working mothers have no childcare option.

~25 children

per creche, 6 days a week, 9am–6pm. Two trained caregivers, one in-charge. Daily hot meal, structured play, growth monitoring, school readiness.

Mother → work

A working mother earns ₹150–250/day. Without childcare she stays home or loses 1 day/week to a sick child unattended. The creche is also an income unlock.

Then anganwadi/govt

At 3, we mainstream the child into the local anganwadi or government school. The creche is a bridge, not a parallel system. Our job ends when the State takes over.

A creche fails when the caregiver is wrong, not when the building is wrong. Read every pitstop with that lens — caregiver quality is what most of these steps are protecting.

A DAY AT THE CRECHE

What ~25 children experience, 9am to 6pm

If any of these blocks is missing or rushed, that day stops counting toward the child's development.

9:00–9:30
Drop-off, handwash, attendance, mood check
9:30–10:30
Free-play with age-appropriate materials; caregiver observation log
10:30–11:00
Morning snack — banana + milk or millet ladoo. Wash and seat in groups.
11:00–12:30
Structured early-learning activity (storytelling, songs, motor-skill play). Age-segregated.
12:30–1:30
Hot meal — rice, dal, vegetable, egg (or substitute). Plate-clearance and brushing follow.
1:30–3:00
Nap time. Each child has a mat and a clean sheet from home.
3:00–3:30
Wake, freshen, evening snack — sprouts/peanuts
3:30–5:00
Outdoor play if weather allows; otherwise indoor group games and finger-painting
5:00–5:45
Quiet activity — looking at picture books, gentle music — wind-down
5:45–6:00
Pickup, hand-over of day notes to mother, growth-monitoring entry if it is the weekly day
THE CAREGIVER LOOP

Hire → train → observe → coach — repeat monthly

A caregiver who feels watched, supported, and respected stays. A creche stands or falls on this loop running every month.

🧭
Hire from community
SHG member · class-10 minimum · lives in/near the settlement · trusted by mothers · RP final interview
→
🎓
Pre-service train
5-day residential · child development basics · safety · nutrition · first-aid · activity planning · positive discipline
→
👀
Observe weekly
CO visits 2× a week · child mood + caregiver tone · meal hygiene · activity quality · safety check
→
💬
Coach monthly
Caregiver supervisor 1:1 · review observation notes · pick 1 thing to improve · revisit next month

↻ Why the loop closes back to coaching, not firing: caregivers in slum settings face stresses we don't (sick children at home, husband out of work, water shortages). Pure performance management produces high churn. Coaching produces low churn and steadily improving practice. Replace only when coaching has demonstrably not worked for 3 months.
