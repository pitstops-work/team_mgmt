WHY THIS GOAL MATTERS

Existing partners drift when reviews go on autopilot

Quarterly review + audit + annual check are not paperwork — they are the only structured space where the relationship gets honest.

Quarterly review

Both sides bring data · review against MoU outcomes · one set of action items · 90 min · agenda fixed

Deliverable audit

Spot-check claimed deliverables · field verify with beneficiaries · catches the 20% of partners drifting from spec

Relationship temp

Direct conversation: what's frustrating you about us · what's working · candid signal beats survey

Annual health

Written report · governance + financial + programmatic + relationship · used in renewal decision

QUARTERLY REVIEW STRUCTURE

90 min · 4 blocks · co-owned outcomes

0–20 min
Outcomes scoreboard · MoU metrics · both sides agree on numbers before any discussion
20–50 min
What worked · what didn't · honest conversation · no defending the data
50–75 min
Joint problem-solving · unblockers · escalations to senior level
75–90 min
Next-quarter commitments · owner + date · circulated within 48 hrs
RED-AMBER-GREEN HEALTH

When to renew · when to coach · when to wind down

🟢 Green — renew
  • · MoU outcomes 80%+ delivered
  • · Compliance clean · audit unqualified
  • · Field data + own MIS aligned
  • · Reviews on time, agenda-driven
🟡 Amber — coach
  • · 50–80% MoU delivery
  • · Compliance gaps but in fix-mode
  • · Some MIS drift · cohort visible
  • · Reviews happening but unfocused
🔴 Red — wind down
  • · < 50% delivery 2 quarters running
  • · Compliance failure · audit qualified
  • · MIS-field mismatch persistent
  • · Trust collapse · governance breach
PITSTOP 1 OF 3 · MEETING · 90 DAYS

Quarterly Partner Joint Review

Why this pitstop

Quarterly review with all 2 partner(s). Review joint deliverables, address friction, and plan the next quarter. Be honest about what is and isn't working.

1.
All 2 partner(s) present at review
2.
Previous quarter deliverables reviewed against plan
3.
Data quality and reporting timeliness discussed
4.
Financial utilisation reviewed (if funds are involved)
5.
Friction points surfaced and resolved or escalated
6.
Next quarter joint plan confirmed
7.
Minutes documented and shared within 5 days
PITSTOP 2 OF 3 · REVIEW · 85 DAYS

Partner Deliverable & Reporting Audit

Why this pitstop

Quality audit of what partners have actually delivered vs. committed. Don't conflate activity (meetings held, visits done) with outcome (children enrolled, cases resolved).

1.
Deliverable log updated for all 2 partner(s)
2.
Commitments vs. actuals documented
3.
Evidence of key deliverables collected (reports, photos, beneficiary data)
4.
Variance between committed and delivered noted with explanation
5.
Financial utilisation vs. milestones verified
6.
Corrective actions agreed where needed
7.
Audit summary shared with manager
PITSTOP 3 OF 3 · REVIEW · 365 DAYS

Annual Partnership Health Review

Why this pitstop

Annual 360° review of each partnership. Is it working? Is the partner delivering? Is the relationship healthy? This informs renewal, scale-up, or exit decisions.

1.
Annual outcome data compiled for all 2 partner(s)
2.
Health check conversation held with each partner (frank, two-way)
3.
Partner satisfaction with the relationship assessed
4.
Our satisfaction with the partner's delivery assessed
5.
Renewal, scale-up, or exit recommendation drafted per partner
6.
Recommendation shared with Director
7.
Decision communicated to partner with adequate notice
