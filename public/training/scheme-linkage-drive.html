PITSTOP 1 OF 10 · TRAINING · 21 DAYS

Team Deployment & Scheme Training

Why this pitstop

Assign and train the field team for 1,250 households. Staff needed: 1 Programme Manager, 2 Community Organiser(s) (1 per 625 HH), 1 Area Coordinator(s) (1 per 3–4 COs), 1 Resource Centre Coordinator(s) (1 per RCC), 1 MIS Coordinator. Training covers: eligibility criteria for each scheme, required documents, application process (online/offline), common rejection reasons, how to update household MIS, and daily plan generation logic.

1.
Assign Programme Manager (1 overall)
2.
Assign 2 Community Organiser(s) (1 per 625 HH)
3.
Assign 1 Area Coordinator(s) (1 per 3–4 COs)
4.
Assign 1 Resource Centre Coordinator(s) (1 per RCC)
5.
Assign MIS Coordinator (1 overall)
6.
Conduct 3-day induction: scheme eligibility, document checklist, application process
7.
Train on CMCHIS / PMJAY: eligibility (income <₹72k/year), docs (Aadhaar + Income cert), 1467 empanelled hospitals
8.
Train on PMJJBY: ₹2L life insurance @₹436/year, needs bank account, age 18–50
9.
Train on PMSBY: ₹2L accident insurance @₹20/year, needs bank account, age 18–70
10.
Train on APY: pension ₹1k–5k/month on retirement, age 18–40, bank account needed
11.
Train on state welfare pensions: old age (60+), widow, disability (UDID required)
12.
Train on PMAY: housing subsidy for homeless/kachha house, Aadhaar + income cert + land doc
13.
Train on NFSA / ration card: PHH (5 kg/person @₹3/kg), AAY for destitute
14.
Train on Jan Dhan: zero-balance account, RuPay card, Aadhaar seeding for DBT
15.
Identify and note key government contacts per scheme: taluk office, CMCHIS help desk, bank BC agent
16.
Set up team WhatsApp group for daily updates and issue escalation
PITSTOP 2 OF 10 · MILESTONE · 35 DAYS

Settlement Mapping & Household Linelisting

Why this pitstop

Build a complete household register for the settlement. Sketch or GPS-map each street/lane. Enumerate every household: head name, family members (name/age/gender), address, and contact number. This linelist is the foundation of all subsequent scheme-tracking work.

1.
Prepare or verify settlement map — identify all streets/lanes/blocks
2.
Assign streets to each CO for systematic door-to-door coverage
3.
Enumerate all households: head of household name, address, mobile number
4.
List all family members: name, age, gender, relationship to head
5.
Note if household is tenant or owner; approximate house structure (pucca/kachha/kutcha)
6.
Flag vulnerable households: elderly (60+) living alone, widows, PwD, single women HH, destitute
7.
Enter all households into MIS (Frappe / field app)
8.
Review coverage — ensure no street/lane missed; verify count against any existing community data
9.
Confirm final linelist count against target (1250 HH)
PITSTOP 3 OF 10 · MILESTONE · 50 DAYS

Baseline Entitlement Survey

Why this pitstop

Survey every household to map current entitlement status and document gaps. Per-household fields: CMCHIS/PMJAY status, ration card type (AAY/PHH/SPHH/none), Jan Dhan bank account, social security schemes enrolled (PMJJBY/PMSBY/APY), pension status, housing type, voter ID status, and which Aadhaar/income/caste certificates are present. This drives scheme-wise priority lists in the MIS.

1.
Design or finalise baseline survey form — cover all schemes and document fields
2.
Conduct survey for all listed households (attach to existing linelist records)
3.
Record per HH: ration card number and type (AAY / PHH / SPHH / none)
4.
Record per HH: CMCHIS / PMJAY status (Active / Applied / Not Applied)
5.
Record per individual: Jan Dhan or bank account (Yes/No), account number if yes
6.
Record per individual: PMJJBY enrolled (Y/N), PMSBY enrolled (Y/N), APY enrolled (Y/N)
7.
Record per eligible elderly/widow/PwD: pension status and pension amount if receiving
8.
Record per individual: voter ID present (Y/N), correct address (Y/N)
9.
Record per HH: house type (pucca/semi-pucca/kachha) — flag kachha/homeless for PMAY
10.
Record per individual: Aadhaar present (Y/N), mobile seeded (Y/N), name/DOB correct (Y/N)
11.
Record per individual: income certificate present (Y/N), date of issue
12.
Record per individual: caste/community certificate present (Y/N) where applicable
13.
Update MIS with survey data; generate scheme-wise gap reports
14.
Brief Area Coordinators on household priority lists per scheme
PITSTOP 4 OF 10 · MILESTONE · 80 DAYS

Document Foundation Drive (Aadhaar, Income Cert, Ration Card, Jan Dhan)

Why this pitstop

Documents are the common bottleneck across all schemes. Prioritise this before scheme-specific drives. Key work: Aadhaar corrections and mobile seeding, Income Certificate applications and renewals (4-day ETA at TN taluk offices), Ration Card family member updates, Jan Dhan zero-balance account opening. Run document camps to batch-process multiple households in a day.

1.
Identify households with Aadhaar gaps from baseline — missing, incorrect name/DOB, mobile not seeded
2.
Organise Aadhaar correction/enrollment camp or schedule CO-accompanied trips to Aadhaar centre
3.
Seed mobile number to Aadhaar for DBT linkage (UIDAI portal or Aadhaar centre)
4.
Identify households needing Income Certificate (new application or renewal/expired)
5.
Batch-apply Income Certificates at taluk office — track ETA (typically 4 days)
6.
Follow up on pending Income Certs — escalate delays beyond 10 days to supervisor
7.
Flag expired Income Certs — re-apply immediately (blocks CMCHIS)
8.
Review ration cards — identify non-members, newborns, new members needing addition
9.
Support ration card family member additions at TNPDS / PDS office
10.
Identify households with AAY eligibility not yet upgraded from PHH — support reclassification
11.
Identify adults without Jan Dhan/bank account; coordinate with nearest bank BC agent or branch
12.
Open zero-balance Jan Dhan accounts; ensure RuPay debit card issued
13.
Link Aadhaar to bank account for DBT (required for pension, PMAY, PMJJBY/PMSBY auto-debit)
14.
Track document status per household/individual in MIS — update after each completion
PITSTOP 5 OF 10 · MILESTONE · 100 DAYS

Health Insurance Enrollment Drive (CMCHIS / PMJAY)

Why this pitstop

Primary health insurance enrollment for all eligible households. CMCHIS (TN): ₹5 lakh/year per family, requires Aadhaar + Income Cert (income <₹72,000/year), 1,467 empanelled hospitals. PMJAY (Ayushman Bharat): ₹5 lakh/year for SECC-listed households — check beneficiary status on PMJAY portal. Run CO daily plan (30 HH/day) prioritising doc-ready households. Track: not applied → applied → active; rejected → re-applied.

1.
Generate MIS list: households with Aadhaar Received AND Income Cert Received — highest priority
2.
Run CO daily plan (30 HH/day) starting with doc-ready households
3.
Accompany or guide HH head to nearest CMCHIS enrollment centre with documents
4.
Check PMJAY beneficiary status for each HH on pmjay.gov.in — eligible HH enrolled directly
5.
Record CMCHIS / PMJAY application number and expected activation date in MIS
6.
Follow up on Applied HH after 5-day ETA — check activation status
7.
Investigate rejected cases: reason for rejection (income limit, name mismatch, duplicate)
8.
Correct docs for rejected cases and re-apply — track as second application in MIS
9.
Inform activated families: how to use card, nearest empanelled hospitals, cashless process
10.
Notify Aadhaar-pending and Income-cert-pending HH of their blocking item — route to Document Drive
11.
Track overall pipeline: Not Applied / Docs Pending / Applied / Active / Rejected
PITSTOP 6 OF 10 · MILESTONE · 120 DAYS

Social Security Scheme Drive (PMJJBY / PMSBY / APY)

Why this pitstop

Bank-account-linked social security for individuals — best enrolled in a camp with bank BC agent. PMJJBY: ₹2L life insurance @₹436/year auto-debited, age 18–50, bank account needed. PMSBY: ₹2L accident insurance @₹20/year, age 18–70, bank account needed. APY: state pension ₹1k–5k/month on retirement based on contribution, age 18–40, bank account needed. These are individual-level enrollments — target every eligible adult.

1.
Generate MIS list: adults aged 18–50 with bank account but not enrolled in PMJJBY
2.
Generate MIS list: adults aged 18–70 with bank account but not enrolled in PMSBY
3.
Generate MIS list: adults aged 18–40 in informal sector — APY candidates
4.
Coordinate with nearest bank BC agent or branch to run enrollment camp in the settlement
5.
Enroll eligible adults in PMJJBY — fill nomination form, confirm auto-debit set up
6.
Enroll eligible adults in PMSBY — same session, ₹20/year, confirm auto-debit
7.
Enroll interested youth/adults in APY — explain contribution tiers (₹42–₹291/month) and pension amounts
8.
Update MIS: PMJJBY enrolled (Y/N), PMSBY enrolled (Y/N), APY enrolled (Y/N) per individual
9.
Brief families: what PMJJBY covers (death/natural cause), claim process, nominee importance
10.
Brief families: what PMSBY covers (accident death + disability), claim process
11.
Follow up: confirm bank statements show first auto-debit for PMJJBY/PMSBY
PITSTOP 7 OF 10 · MILESTONE · 130 DAYS

Welfare Pension & Disability Entitlements Drive

Why this pitstop

State and central pension schemes for elderly, widows, and persons with disability. Old Age Pension (TN IGNOAPS): ₹1,000/month for age 60+, income-poor households — apply at Village Administrative Officer (VAO) or Taluk office. Widow Pension (IGNWPS): ₹1,000/month, apply similarly. UDID (Unique Disability ID): national disability card enabling reservations, travel concessions, scheme access — requires govt medical board assessment.

1.
Identify elderly (60+) not receiving any pension from baseline survey
2.
Verify income eligibility — old age pension targets landless, income-poor HH
3.
Support application at Taluk office / VAO: Aadhaar, bank passbook, age proof, income cert
4.
Identify widows not receiving widow pension — support IGNWPS application
5.
Track pension applications: submitted → approved → first payment received in MIS
6.
Identify PwD (persons with disability) from baseline — confirm disability type
7.
Support visit to nearest govt hospital for disability certificate / medical board assessment
8.
Facilitate UDID card enrollment at udid.gov.in after disability certificate issued
9.
Identify PwD with UDID eligible for disability pension — apply at Taluk office
10.
Brief families on UDID benefits: reservations, concessional travel, PDS priority, scheme access
11.
Update MIS: pension status (not applied / applied / approved / receiving) per individual
PITSTOP 8 OF 10 · MILESTONE · 150 DAYS

Housing, Voter ID & Civic Entitlements Drive

Why this pitstop

PMAY (Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana): ₹1.2–2.5L subsidy for homeless or kachha-house families — Aadhaar, income cert, land document needed; apply at panchayat/municipality. Voter ID: enroll unregistered adults (Form 6 at BLO or online on voters.eci.gov.in). Property/Patta: support where settlement has been regularised. Civic gaps (water, sanitation, street lights) — coordinate with WRP/Welfare Rights team for systemic follow-up.

1.
Identify households in kachha/kutcha house or homeless from baseline — flag for PMAY
2.
Check if settlement appears in PMAY beneficiary list (AwaasSoft portal or municipality)
3.
Support PMAY application: Aadhaar, income cert, photo, bank account, land/possession document
4.
Track PMAY applications: applied → approved → construction linked payment (CLP)
5.
Identify adults without voter ID or with wrong address — support Form 6 or correction application
6.
Conduct voter ID enrollment/correction camp (or schedule BLO visit to settlement)
7.
Track voter ID status per adult in MIS (not enrolled / applied / received)
8.
Identify households without piped water connection — document and hand over to civic action team
9.
Identify households without individual toilet / open defecation — SBM linkage if settlement eligible
10.
Identify streets without street lights / road repair needs — escalate to Ward Councillor / municipality
11.
Update MIS: PMAY status, voter ID status, civic gap flags per household
PITSTOP 9 OF 10 · MILESTONE · 180 DAYS

Ongoing Followup & Pipeline Tracking

Why this pitstop

Continuous CO-led household followup driven by MIS priority lists — same model as CMCHIS daily work plan (30 HH/day per CO, pool-based prioritisation). Priority pools: (1) households with zero scheme coverage, (2) households with docs ready but applications pending, (3) households with overdue follow-up / pending status, (4) recently activated — check experience and address issues. Refresh MIS after each visit.

1.
Set up MIS daily plan generation — pools based on scheme coverage gaps and doc readiness
2.
Confirm COs running 30 HH visits/day per daily plan
3.
Weekly review: % households with CMCHIS active, PMJJBY, PMSBY, APY enrolled
4.
Weekly review: income cert pipeline — pending, applied, received, expired
5.
Weekly review: Aadhaar pipeline — missing, correction pending, received
6.
Track rejection rate per scheme — investigate if >10% rejections for any scheme
7.
Track households with zero scheme coverage — these are highest priority
8.
Ensure newborns, new migrants, and newly-identified HH are onboarded to MIS
9.
Handle mid-year Income Cert expiry — re-apply before CMCHIS renewal deadline
10.
Monthly Area Coordinator review: scheme-wise pipeline, CO coverage rate, blockers
PITSTOP 10 OF 10 · MEETING · 180 DAYS

Community Review & Grievance Redressal

Why this pitstop

Monthly community meetings to share entitlement progress and collect grievances. Grievance types: scheme denial without valid reason, corruption/bribery at government offices, application delays beyond SLA, wrong rejection (name mismatch, manual error). Escalation path: verbal → written complaint → senior official → RTI. Jan Sunwai (public hearing) for systemic denial affecting many households.

1.
Schedule monthly community meeting — share scheme enrollment numbers and pending list
2.
Collect grievances: households denied schemes, bribery demands, application delays
3.
Categorise grievances: individual (name mismatch, doc issue) vs systemic (policy/process gap)
4.
Resolve individual cases: write application to concerned officer with supporting docs
5.
Escalate systemic issues to District Officer / Collector with documented evidence
6.
Track grievance status in MIS: raised → escalated → resolved
7.
File RTI application for scheme-specific denial data if systemic pattern identified
8.
Organise Jan Sunwai (public hearing) if large number of HH denied same scheme
9.
Document success stories — households that received CMCHIS and actually used it at hospital
10.
Share quarterly progress report with programme leadership: scheme coverage %, pending, grievances
