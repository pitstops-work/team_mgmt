PITSTOP 1 OF 5 · RESEARCH · 14 DAYS

Needs Assessment & Funder Alignment — Funder

Why this pitstop

Establish why Funder is the right fit and what the proposal should address. Confirm the funder's priorities, geographic focus, and funding envelope before committing staff time to a full proposal.

1.
Review Funder's published guidelines, focus areas, and grant ceiling
2.
Map our programme gaps to funder priorities
3.
Confirm there is no active grant agreement that would restrict new funding from this source
4.
Establish contact with programme officer (if possible)
5.
Collect community-level data to quantify the need
6.
Document alignment rationale (1-page internal note)
7.
Get internal sign-off to proceed with proposal
PITSTOP 2 OF 5 · PROPOSAL · 28 DAYS

Concept Note / Letter of Intent — Funder

Why this pitstop

Submit a concise concept note or letter of intent to Funder. Most funders request this before inviting a full proposal. Aim for 2–3 pages.

1.
Problem statement drafted (data-backed)
2.
Proposed intervention described clearly
3.
Expected outcomes and beneficiary reach stated
4.
Indicative budget range included
5.
Organisation credentials section completed
6.
Internal review done (programme lead sign-off)
7.
Concept note submitted to Funder
8.
Submission acknowledged / reference number obtained
PITSTOP 3 OF 5 · PROPOSAL · 56 DAYS

Renewal / Scale-Up Proposal — Funder

Why this pitstop

Renewal or scale-up proposal for Funder. Lead with outcome data from the previous grant period. Funders expect you to show what changed, not just what you did.

1.
Previous grant outcomes compiled and verified (beneficiary data, story count)
2.
Learning from previous period documented
3.
Proposed scale or focus shift clearly justified
4.
Updated implementation plan drafted
5.
Revised budget prepared (include learnings from previous period's spend)
6.
M&E framework updated
7.
Internal review done
8.
Renewal proposal submitted to Funder
9.
Submission confirmation saved
PITSTOP 4 OF 5 · MEETING · 90 DAYS

Post-Submission Funder Engagement — Funder

Why this pitstop

Keep the relationship alive during the review period. Respond promptly to any queries. Do not go silent after submission.

1.
Funder queries responded to within 48 hours
2.
Clarification calls or site visits accommodated
3.
Updated financials or additional documents provided if requested
4.
Internal tracking note updated with funder interactions
5.
Decision timeline confirmed with programme officer
PITSTOP 5 OF 5 · REVIEW · 110 DAYS

Grant Decision & Agreement Execution — Funder

Why this pitstop

Handle the grant decision — whether approval, rejection, or conditional approval. If approved, execute the grant agreement and set up internal compliance tracking.

1.
Decision received from Funder
2.
If approved: grant agreement reviewed by Director and legal
3.
If approved: grant agreement signed and counter-signed
4.
If approved: compliance and reporting schedule extracted and calendared
5.
If approved: finance notified and project code created
6.
If approved: programme team briefed on deliverables and reporting timelines
7.
If rejected: debrief with funder requested
8.
If rejected: lessons documented for future applications
