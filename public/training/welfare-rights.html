WHY THIS GOAL MATTERS

Most slum households are eligible for entitlements they cannot access

The schemes exist. The funds exist. The forms exist. What's missing is the bridge — and that bridge is community organising.

~14 schemes

a typical slum household qualifies for — ration, pension, scholarship, maternity benefit, health insurance, housing, water connection, sanitation subsidy. Most households access 2–3.

Documents first

Every scheme starts with the same three: Aadhaar, income certificate, ration card. Without these foundational IDs, no other entitlement unlocks. That is why we run document drives first.

MAS + leaders

Mahila Arogya Samiti (women's health committee) + community leaders are the unit of change. Individual case-work doesn't scale. The committee scales because it negotiates collectively with the State.

Civic agency

The goal isn't to deliver entitlements one-time. It's to make the community capable of demanding them on its own — every year, from every ward councillor, for every new entrant. We exit; the agency stays.

A welfare rights programme that hands out scheme benefits without building the MAS and leader bench has produced nothing of lasting value. Every checklist here is in service of the leaders, not the benefits.

DOCUMENTS FOUNDATION

The pyramid: foundational IDs unlock everything above

Why we drive Aadhaar / income cert / ration card / Jan Dhan before any scheme application.

🏠
Housing rights (PMAY) · land title deed · in-situ rehabilitation
🎓
Education entitlements · pre-matric / post-matric scholarship · RTE admission
🏥
Health insurance (Ayushman / CMCHIS) · maternity benefit · disability pension
🍚
Ration entitlement (PDS) · Antyodaya · widow pension · old-age pension
🆔
Aadhaar · Income Certificate · Ration Card · Jan Dhan account
↑ Each tier requires every layer below it ↑

A household without an Aadhaar-linked ration card cannot enrol in PDS. Without PDS history, no income certificate. Without income certificate, no scholarship, no pension, no Ayushman. Start at the base of the pyramid; never skip a tier.

THE ECOSYSTEM

Who does what — and who hands work off to whom

The programme is a layered network. Every layer has a defined role; failures are usually a missing handoff, not a missing role.

Our team
RP + Community Organisers (COs)
  • · Settlement mapping
  • · CO training + supervision
  • · Liaison with ULB / govt
  • · Issue-based campaigns
  • · MIS quality + reporting
Community structures
MAS + Community Leaders
  • · Women's health committee (MAS)
  • · Settlement-level leader (≥1 per cluster)
  • · Identify household-level needs
  • · Lead door-to-door drives
  • · Negotiate with ward councillor
State / Govt
Ward councillor · Tehsildar · BBMP/CMC officer
  • · Issues documents (Aadhaar / income cert)
  • · Approves scheme applications
  • · Allocates ward-level funds
  • · Owns long-term service delivery
Critical handoffs
RP / CO → MAS: document drive, issue-based meeting agenda, training calendar. CO is present; MAS chairs.
MAS → Govt: formal letter from the committee (not from us). Demand is a community demand, not an NGO ask.
Govt → Household: document or scheme benefit reaches the household. CO logs in MIS; MAS verifies.
