WHY THIS GOAL MATTERS

A single, mutually reinforcing crisis of bodily dignity

Open defecation, unsafe bathing, no laundry, no clean drinking water — addressing any one in isolation has consistently failed.

that women in Chennai slums spend fetching water and managing laundry — a direct opportunity cost an integrated complex can eliminate.

2,000–5,000 mg/L

6.5 M litres/yr

The toilet block alone, the bathing block alone, the water ATM alone — each fails in two years without the others. The complex works because the services co-locate, share infrastructure, and create one operational model that one CMC can govern.

PITSTOP 1 OF 9 · RESEARCH · 28 DAYS

Site, Land Tenure & Water Source Assessment

Why this pitstop

The single most common killer of community sanitation projects is land tenure. RP secures a formal lease or MoU (15+ years minimum) before any capital is committed. Site must be within 200–300 metres of all target households — beyond that distance, communities revert to open defecation even with a working facility nearby. Water source assessment runs in parallel: the complex needs 15,000–25,000 L/day with greywater recycling; double that without.

1.
RP identifies candidate plot (300–500 sq m minimum) within 200–300 metres walking distance of all target households
🎙Candidate Site Survey
2.
RP secures formal land agreement (15+ year lease / MoU) with BBMP / TNSCB / ULB / landowner — no capital committed before this is signed
📎Land MoU / Lease Document
3.
Confirm sewer line proximity for blackwater disposal; if absent, plan for septic tank + FSTP hookup or biodigester
✓Sewer / Drainage Network Survey
4.
Borewell or municipal water source assessed: TDS, hardness, microbial; full NABL lab panel if borewell will be used
📎Water Source Lab Test Report
5.
Confirm commercial electrical connection available (single or 3-phase); plan backup (stabiliser / UPS / solar)
✓Electrical Supply Confirmation
6.
Avoid sites in flood-prone low-lying areas (monsoon inundation risk) or adjacent to open drains / waste dumps (odour, vandalism)
🎙Flood + Hygiene Risk Walk-through
7.
Soil investigation report for G+2 foundation design
📎Soil Test Report
PITSTOP 2 OF 9 · MEETING · 42 DAYS

Community Demand & CMC Formation

Why this pitstop

RPs go door-to-door before any design work. We need real demand: household count, gender-disaggregated willingness-to-pay, peak-hour load. The single best predictor of long-term sustainability is community ownership — formed through the Community Management Committee (CMC). 5–7 members including at least 3 women, an SHG representative, and one elderly or differently-abled member. CMC governs tariff changes, complaints, and operator renewal.

1.
Door-to-door household count and OD prevalence survey within service radius
📎Household Demand Survey
2.
Willingness-to-pay survey at multiple price points (Re.1 / ₹2 / ₹3 per use; ₹100–200 / month pass)
📎Willingness-to-Pay Survey
3.
Gender-disaggregated focus groups on bathing / laundry / safety needs — women’s inputs drive design
🎙Women’s Focus Group
4.
Identify peak-hour windows (5–8 AM, 8–10 PM) and size for peak load
✓Peak Usage Mapping
5.
Form Community Management Committee (5–7 members; ≥3 women; SHG rep; elderly / differently-abled rep)
🎙CMC Formation Meeting
6.
CMC drafts and signs governance charter (tariff approval, complaint adjudication, operator review process)
📎CMC Charter
7.
RP verifies CMC membership and conducts orientation on roles, dashboard reading, and decision cadence
🎙CMC Orientation
PITSTOP 3 OF 9 · PROPOSAL · 56 DAYS

Regulatory Clearances & Funding

Why this pitstop

Six clearances run in parallel; any one held up blocks construction. SBM-U 2.0 grant (₹98,000 / WC seat + ₹32,000 / urinal from GoI) is the primary capital window; AMRUT 2.0 covers water / sewerage / FSSM. Additional gap funding typically covers 30–40% of capex. Submit the SBM application early — it has long review cycles.

FSSAI registration applied for the Water ATM service component
📎FSSAI Registration
4.
CGWB / SGWB NoC for new borewell (if applicable)
📎CGWB Borewell NoC
5.
Fire NoC for G+2 structure from state fire department
📎Fire NoC
6.
SBM-U 2.0 grant application submitted via BBMP / TNSCB
📎SBM-U 2.0 Application

PITSTOP 4 OF 9 · BUDGETING · 84 DAYS

Architectural Design & Procurement

Why this pitstop

G+2 building: ground floor WC blocks (separate male / female + child + differently-abled), first floor bathing + laundromat, second floor RO water + greywater plant + storage tanks. Anti-slip R11 floor tiles mandatory. Plumbing must keep blackwater and greywater on separate lines from day one — retrofit is near-impossible. Procure industrial-grade washing machines rated for 8–10 hours daily continuous operation; domestic machines fail within weeks under community use intensity.

📎Laundry Equipment PO

PITSTOP 6 OF 9 · MILESTONE · 175 DAYS

Commissioning & Compliance Testing

Why this pitstop

48-hour soak test runs every system at design load. Water quality tests for both the RO product water (IS 10500 BIS standards) and greywater effluent (BOD <10 mg/L, turbidity <5 NTU per IS 16101) must pass before any user is admitted. FSSAI sample submission is mandatory for the water ATM service. Skip none of this; commissioning gaps surface as community trust collapses later.

1.
48-hour continuous operational test of all systems (toilets flushing, showers, washing machines, RO, greywater)
📎48-hr Soak Test Log
2.
RO product water tested against IS 10500 BIS standards at NABL lab (TDS, microbial, chemical)
📎RO Product Water Test
3.
Greywater effluent tested: BOD <10 mg/L, turbidity <5 NTU, E. coli not detected (IS 16101)
📎Greywater Effluent Test
4.
FSSAI water sample submission for pre-operation compliance
📎FSSAI Sample Submission
5.
All payment modes tested end-to-end (RFID swipe, UPI QR, coin if installed, AePS if installed)
✓Payment System E2E Test
6.
Safety walk-through: night lighting working, door latches functional, ventilation adequate, anti-slip floor verified
🎙Safety Walk-through
7.
RP signs commissioning report — facility ready for soft launch
📎Commissioning Sign-off

PITSTOP 8 OF 9 · MEETING · 224 DAYS

Soft Launch & Household Registration

Why this pitstop

A soft launch with subsidised pricing breaks the first-use barrier — the same logic as RFID free-trial credit at water ATMs. Door-to-door household registration with RFID + monthly pass issuance is the single most reliable predictor of sustained adoption. Free-use demo day generates word-of-mouth; without it, uptake stalls.

1.
Free-use demo day with full-service demonstration (toilet, bath, laundry, drinking water); CMC + ward councillor invited
🎙Free-Use Demo Day
2.
Door-to-door household registration; RFID cards + monthly passes issued with photo + household ID
📎Household Registration Log
3.
Subsidised trial pricing (4–6 weeks at 50% rate) to break first-use barrier
✓Trial Pricing Window
4.
Agent network activated: 2–4 community agents (SHG / shopkeeper) trained to sell + recharge passes on commission
✓Agent Network Activation
5.
Free / social quota programmed in payment system: 10–15% of daily capacity reserved for verified extreme-poverty / disability / child users
✓Social Quota Configuration
6.
Weekly check-ins for first 4 weeks; CMC + RP debrief on adoption, complaints, queue patterns
🎙Week-1 to Week-4 Debrief

COMMON FAILURE MODES

Why complexes fail — and what stops the failure

Every checklist in this template exists because a previous project failed without it.

👩
Women stop using after 2–3 months
Cause: Poor lighting · no working latches · no privacy · safety
Prevention: LED motion-sensor lighting · heavy-duty lever locks · separate entrances on different faces · night guard
🚱
Foul odour, hygienic decline
Cause: Insufficient cleaning shifts · blocked drains · failing water seal traps
Prevention: 3 cleaning shifts/day · drain inspection daily · smell = blocked trap = fix within 2 hrs
💸
Revenue leakage
Cause: Cash pilferage · meter fraud · operator bypass
Prevention: RFID/UPI dominance · IoT footfall counting · daily reconciliation · shift cash audit
🧨
Vandalism / equipment theft
Cause: No community ownership · isolated location · poor siting
Prevention: CMC stewardship · CCTV · busy-node siting · monthly community feedback meetings
🚱
Water supply failure
Cause: Municipal supply cut · borewell depletion · pump failure
Prevention: 2-day storage buffer · tanker backup MoU · duty-standby pump arrangement
🚫
Exclusion of poorest
Cause: High pricing · no free tier · payment-system barriers
Prevention: Free-use quota (10–15%) · cash always accepted · SHG subsidy pass · cross-tier pricing
⚙️
Operator departure
Cause: Low wages · no growth path · burnout
Prevention: Fair contracts · livelihood training · operator pathway to manage multiple sites
🪣
Septic tank overflow
Cause: Infrequent desludging · under-sizing
Prevention: Size for 125% of peak load · register with municipal desludging schedule (every 3–5 yrs)
🌧
Facility abandoned in 2 yrs
Cause: No community ownership · operator dropout · management vacuum
Prevention: CMC oversight · operator contract with performance incentives · community-driven design
