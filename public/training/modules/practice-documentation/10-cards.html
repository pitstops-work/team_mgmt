Quick-Reference Cards

Print & Keep · One Card per Audience

All audiences Print on A4, fold, laminate · or keep as a phone wallpaper

The curriculum compressed to a single glance, one card per audience. Owner, CO, RP, ZL, Curator. Each card carries the five accountabilities, the SLAs, and the anti-patterns to watch for. Use when in doubt; refer back to the full chapter when wrestling with a specific call.

Owner
6-month term · 1 page

Five accountabilities

  • The page is current — reflects what good COs do today.
  • The practice circle happens on its date, even after rotation.
  • Flags acknowledged in 7 days, resolved in 30.
  • 90-day review is on the calendar and gets done.
  • 6-month handover is real — listen-tour, note, conversations.

The clock

  • Months 1–3: you facilitate the circle.
  • Month 4+: rotation among COs. You scribe.
  • Month 3 & 6: quarterly page walk.
  • Month 6: handover steps in order.

SLAs

48h trigger → draft 7d acknowledge flag 30d resolve flag 14d respond to comment

Anti-patterns — watch for in yourself

  • The silent term — no circles, no edits, no flags handled.
  • The owner monologue — you explain instead of comparing.
  • Aspiration drift — edits describe what should be.
  • The bunker — declining flags without bringing them to the circle.

One rule

  • If the page disagrees with what good COs actually do — the page is wrong, not the COs.
CO
In the room · in the field

What you bring

  • The field, into the room. Compare what your partner does to others.
  • One surprise per month to the surprise log.
  • A flag whenever the page is wrong, missing, or unclear.
  • A gap whenever no page exists for what you are doing.

Gap vs flag

  • Flag = "this page is wrong." File on a page.
  • Gap = "no page covers this." File without a page.
  • If unsure: search 1 minute first, then decide.

In the circle

  • Open round — your one surprise this month.
  • Case discussion — say how your partner does it; disagree politely.
  • Synthesis — agree, push back, or sharpen the proposed edit.
  • Vernacular default — don't switch to English for senior people.

What is not yours

  • Drafting the page (owner does that).
  • Closing flags (owner does that).
  • Translating the page (translation queue handles it).
  • Apologising for raising a flag.
RP · Resource Person
Our staff · across partners

Monthly rhythm

  • ≥ 1 shadow visit per partner per quarter.
  • Attend every practice circle for your verticals.
  • Run new-CO onboarding sessions for partners you cover.
  • Hold the partner-review calendar — quarterly per partner.

Shadow visit protocol

  • Tell the CO: "I am here to watch how it actually runs."
  • Walk the full activity end to end.
  • Note three columns: playbook says · CO did · why differ.
  • Do not correct in the moment unless dignity / safety.
  • 48h: draft edit or gap entry.

If you own a page

  • Same five accountabilities as the Owner card.
  • Months 1–3 you facilitate the circle yourself.
  • Stagger your principle term-ends across the calendar.

Red flags to watch

  • A vertical with no circle in 2+ months.
  • A partner where the review meeting has been postponed twice.
  • A shadow visit that produced no edit — was it a social visit?
ZL · Zonal Leader
Dashboard · weekly check

What you watch

  • Owners with flags > 30 days unresolved.
  • Pages stale > 90 days (AMBER) or > 180 days (RED / orphaned).
  • Circles that did not run two cycles in a row.
  • Gap-queue depth crossing 20; median age crossing 14 days.
  • Facilitator rotation index < 3 after the owner's 3-month period.

When you intervene

  • Silent owner past 30d → conversation, possibly early term-end.
  • Repeat circle cancellation → check with owner; merge or sunset.
  • Curator escalation → resolve within a week.

What you do NOT do

  • Edit pages directly. Pull the owner in.
  • Attend every circle. Read the minutes.
  • Triage gaps. Curator does.
  • Translate. Translation queue.

Once a month

  • File any audit-comparison flags from pitstop-runtime patterns.
  • Confirm next month's circles and partner reviews are on calendars.
Curator
1 per city · same day each week

Weekly walks (~ 30 min each)

  • Gap queue. For each open gap: assign · merge · decline.
  • Stale-page sweep. Nudge AMBER owners; flip 180-day rows to orphaned.
  • Flag triage. When owner is silent > 7d, escalate to ZL.
  • Translation queue. Assign open translation flags to translators.

Gap triage decisions

  • Assign → owner + deadline (runbook 7d, playbook 14d).
  • Merge → into an existing page; file a flag for clarification.
  • Decline → with a one-line reason; filer is notified.
  • Default to assign. Decline rate ≤ 15%.

What you never do

  • Edit a page without the owner's consent.
  • Delete a comment. Resolve with a visible summary.
  • Skip the weekly walk. Stale pages compound silently.

Health targets

  • Open gaps < 20.
  • Median gap age < 14 days.
  • Gap-to-publish median < 45 days.
  • Orphaned pages < 5% of corpus.
Trainer · Workshop
Day 1 + Day 2

Two days, ~ 4 hours each

  • Day 1 — Foundations + Practice Circles (the mock circle exercise).
  • Day 2 — Other channels + long-cycle disciplines + commitments.
  • Working lunch both days. Owners read their assigned pages.

The mock circle

  • 45 min, real recent anonymisable case.
  • Facilitator: a CO, never the most senior person.
  • You sit at the back. Take notes. Do not intervene.
  • 15 min debrief at the end — what worked, what bent.

The live gap-queue walk

  • For curators specifically.
  • Open the queue projector; walk five real gaps.
  • Decide live: assign · merge · decline. Capture rationale aloud.

Close the workshop with a commitment

  • Each attendee names one circle they will run in 30 days.
  • And one shadow visit they will make in 30 days.
