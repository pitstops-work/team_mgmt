Module 9

Trainer's Guide — How to Run This Curriculum

RP ZL Anyone delivering this module For workshop facilitators

This curriculum has nine modules totalling roughly seven and a half hours of classroom time. It is designed to be delivered over two days for RPs and partner owners together, then re-run in shorter form for each new wave of CO attendees and new owners as needed. This module is the facilitator's runbook.

Mixed audiences are okay

Run the full curriculum once for a mixed audience — owners, RPs, ZLs and partner senior staff together — before splitting into role-specific re-runs. Cross-role exposure builds the shared vocabulary that makes circles work later.

9.2The two-day workshop format

Two days, roughly 4 hours each, with a working lunch on both days. Treat the workshop as a live circle: lead the room into the protocol, do not lecture about it.

9.3The single most important exercise — the mock circle

In Module 3, do not just teach the run-of-show. Run an actual mock circle in the room. 45 minutes (compressed from 90), with a real case from one of the attendees' partners, facilitated by an attendee (not by you). The point is to feel the protocol — the open round, the case discussion, the synthesis, the next-facilitator naming — in the body.

  • Pick a case before the workshop. Brief the case-bringer; ask for a real, recent, anonymisable situation.
  • Pick a facilitator from among the attendees. Ideally a CO who has never facilitated, not the most senior person.
  • You sit at the back. Take notes. Do not intervene unless safety or dignity is breached.
  • Debrief the mock at the end. What worked, what felt awkward, where the protocol bent. 15 minutes.

If the mock works, the room understands circles. If you skip the mock, the protocol stays theoretical.

9.4The single most important exercise for curators — the gap-queue walk

For curators specifically, the most useful exercise is a live triage of the current gap queue in Module 5. Open the queue projector. Walk five real open gaps together. Decide: assign to draft, merge, decline. Capture rationale aloud. Curators learn the role faster from one live walk than from any number of slides.

9.6Hosting and re-running the workshop

  • First run — Bangalore and Chennai, separately, two days each. RPs + incoming owners + partner senior staff together. Eight to twelve attendees per workshop.
  • Quarterly refresh — half-day, all modules condensed. For new joiners; current owners welcome but not required.
  • On-demand — when a new partner joins, run a half-day for their incoming COs and senior staff before they hold their first circle.
  • Annual — full two-day re-run after major changes to the protocol (e.g., when we add a new water source or change the cadence rules). The change drives the re-run.

Hosting in the Pitstops Training Hub

This module sits at /training/modules/practice-documentation.html as a slide deck for the room, and each chapter is its own scrollable page (this page is chapter 9). Attendees can read the chapters before the workshop and during the breaks. Print the quick-reference cards for the physical handout — one per audience (Owner, CO, RP, ZL, Curator, Trainer).

9.7Refresh cadence — when to update this module

  • After any change to the protocol — a new role, a new cadence, a new health metric.
  • After every quarterly retrospective on the wiki system itself (Q1 each year).
  • When the underlying schema changes — a new model added, a relationship renamed.
  • Whenever a workshop reveals a chapter the room consistently gets wrong — that chapter needs sharpening.

This curriculum is a wiki page about how to keep wiki pages alive. It is held to the same standards: 90-day review, 6-month owner term, flags and comments welcome.

Check your understanding

Three questions — answer before you move on

  1. You are running Day 1 of the workshop. Module 3's mock circle is about to start. The most senior person in the room is the partner founder. Who do you ask to facilitate, and why?
  2. A new owner attends only Modules 0, 1, 2, 3 and not the rest. Six months later, their term has gone poorly — flags piled up, no shadow visits, no handover note. Which missed modules best explain each failure?
  3. Two months after the first workshop, the curators ask you to re-run only Module 5 for the new COs in three settlements. How do you compress 60 minutes of content into a 30-minute session for that audience?
