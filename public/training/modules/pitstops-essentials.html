Before you start

The slate has been wiped clean.

What's different from last week:

  • All your goals were redone. The new ones are the only ones that matter. The old mess is gone.
  • Every overdue activity from before was cleaned up. If it's on your list now, it's real and current.
  • Fewer goals, fewer pitstops. What's left is what we agreed you'd actually do.
  • Activities you'll do at one centre on one day are grouped together — not spread across the month like before.
Mandatory from tomorrow

Pitstops is the one source of truth. If it's not in Pitstops, it didn't happen. If Pitstops doesn't say to do it, you don't owe it that day.

Your ZL, PM, and the Leader all look at the same data. Marking things done here is how the org knows you did the work.

In practice

Open the app at the start of your day. Look at Home → Today. Do what it says. Mark it done. That's the loop. The rest of this deck is how to handle the rough edges.

The very first rule

Stay in Operations. Never go into Setup.

The app has two modes — a small switcher at the bottom-left of the sidebar. Operations is yours. Setup is for admins to add goals, edit templates, change geography. You should never need it.

✗ Don't open Setup unless

Something is grievously wrong — your goals are missing entirely, a creche shows up that doesn't exist, your name isn't on a pitstop you definitely own.

Even then: tell your ZL first. Don't poke at templates yourself.

✓ Live in Operations

Four tabs across the top: Home, Activities, Threads, Notifications. That's it. From these you can see and do every piece of work assigned to you.

Plus three tools in the sidebar: Visit calendar, Route planner, Wiki. We'll cover the first two.

If you accidentally land in Setup

You'll see a longer sidebar (Goals, Field Coverage, Programmes, Programme Map…). Click the mode switcher at the bottom of the sidebar to flip back to Operations. No harm done as long as you didn't change anything.

The daily loop

Pitstops tells you, not the other way around.

A pitstop is one unit of work for one creche / centre / settlement. The app shows you which pitstops have work today, what that work is, and what's coming this week. Your job is to open it and do it.

STEP 1
Open Home → Today
First thing in the morning. See what's scheduled.
STEP 2
Go do it

STEP 3

Right in the app. Captures voice / photo / follow-ups in one screen.
STEP 4
Repeat
Next pitstop on the list. Until Today is clear.
The contract

You never have to remember a creche visit. You never have to keep a paper list. If it's a real piece of work, it's a pitstop in your Today. If it's not in your Today, it can wait.

Home → Today

Your cockpit. Open this every morning.

Today shows you three things in order:

  1. Overdue — yellow / amber. Stuff that didn't get done. We'll fix it.
  2. Today — what's on the plan for right now.
  3. Next 7 days — collapsed. Open it to plan your week.

Plus a "Plan your month" banner that takes you to the drag-and-drop visit calendar. We'll come back to that.

Yes, do this every morning

The whole day fits on one screen. 30 seconds to scan. You'll always know exactly what's expected.

Today · Overdue

If something's overdue, you have three buttons.

Every overdue card has three things you can do — pick the one that's true:

  • Done — green button. You actually did it (today, or yesterday but forgot to mark). Tap it. Done.
  • Reschedule — the "⋯" menu. You couldn't do it that day; pick a new day.
  • Add to today — also in the "⋯" menu. You're doing it now. Pulls it onto today's plan.
Don't let it sit

An overdue card from 10 days ago doesn't impress anyone. Either do it, reschedule it, or pull it to today. Always pick one.

When you mark Done

The follow-up dialog. Don't skip it.

When you tap Done on any activity, a small dialog opens. It does two things at once:

  • Confirms the activity is complete — close the dialog and it's logged as Done.
  • Captures any follow-up action points — the small things the visit threw up that need closure later.

Why this matters. Imagine a creche visit where you spot a missing fire blanket. The visit is done — but the fire blanket isn't. The follow-up dialog is where you note that. You'll see it back on the Follow-ups tab tomorrow until it's actually procured.

Add as many as you need

Each follow-up gets its own due date (defaults to a week out). You can change the date, set a priority, even note who's supposed to do it ("creche supervisor", "ZL Ravi"). You own following up.

Home → Follow-ups

Where your loose ends live until they're tied up.

Every follow-up you logged shows up here, sorted into four buckets:

  • Overdue — red. You missed the due date. Same three actions as overdue activities: Done, Reschedule, or push.
  • Today — yellow. Due today.
  • This week — stone. Coming up.
  • Done (last 30d) — historical. Read-only unless you need to reopen one within 14 days.

When does a follow-up show up on Today? When it's overdue or due today. So if you set "Arrange fire blanket" due in a week, you'll see it sitting in This week until that day, then it pops onto Today.

Close them out

Tap Done on a follow-up to mark it closed — add a closure note ("Delivered to SGN supervisor on Jun 28") + optionally a photo of proof. Future-you and your ZL will thank you.

Home → Done log

Receipts of work. Quietly powerful.

Done log is your history — every activity you've marked done, grouped by day, with the voice log or photo or note you attached.

Use it for two things:

  • Memory recall. "When did I last visit Kempapura?" Two seconds in Done log to find out.
  • Review conversations. When you meet your ZL or PM for a review, your Done log is the conversation. Open it together; you've already done the prep.
Quiet superpower

You don't need to write a weekly update document. Your Done log is your weekly update. Marking things done as you go means there's nothing extra to compile.

Visit calendar

Plan your month. Move things around. Avoid travel chaos.

Click Plan your month on the Today tab (or Visit calendar in the sidebar). You'll see every upcoming visit as a draggable card on a month grid.

What this is really for: rearranging the month so you're not bouncing between clusters. If you've got two Yeshwanthpur creches and one Peenya creche all scheduled for Tuesday, you can drag the Peenya one to Thursday and group your Yeshwanthpur travel together.

  • Drag a visit to another day — the whole pitstop (and every activity under it) moves with it. Activities' time-of-day is preserved.
  • Click a visit to open the reschedule modal explicitly — useful on mobile or if drag feels fiddly.
  • Click "Open" on a card to deep-link to the pitstop detail page.
Set the month, then forget the calendar

Spend 5 minutes on this view at the start of the month. After that, Today shows you what's right and you don't need to revisit the planner.

Activities page

Day / Week / Month — the full calendar.

The Activities page (top nav) shows every activity you're tagged in, in a real calendar layout. Three views: Day, Week, Month — switch at the top.

When to use Activities vs Today:

  • Today — every morning. Quick scan of what's due.
  • Activities — when you want the full month at a glance, or to drag-reschedule, or to find a specific past event in a specific week.

Same activities, prettier view. Anything you can do on Today, you can do here too — Done button on every card, reschedule via drag.

Centre work is grouped

Because the templates now collapse a creche visit's 8 activities onto one day, you'll see all 8 sitting on that one cell — not spread across the month. Same in Today, same in the Activities calendar.

+ New activity

Need to add something the template missed? Yes, you can.

The + New activity button is on every Home tab and on the Activities page. Use it when there's a real piece of work that wasn't pre-planned — a callback meeting, a one-off training, an unscheduled audit.

The dialog asks you to pin it to:

  • Goal — required. Which programme is this work for?
  • Pitstop — required. Which workstream under that goal?
  • Checklist item — optional but recommended. Picks the specific thing on the pitstop's checklist.

Pinning matters: it means your one-off activity rolls up into the right report. If you skip pinning, it floats and doesn't count.

Don't create activities to "log" past work

Use voice log or photo on an existing activity instead. New activities are for new pieces of work going forward.

Tag attendees · Bring people in

An activity isn't only yours. Tag who's coming.

Every activity has an attendees field. By default it's just you. Add anyone who's coming along — partner staff, your ZL, a peer RP — and they get notified + the activity shows on their calendar too.

JOINT VISIT

ZL or PM joining you on a creche walk-through? Tag them. They'll see it on their Today.

CLARIFICATION

Not sure how to complete a specific check? Reach out to your ZL in the activity's thread (chat icon on the card). They get the question with full context.

PARTNER STAFF

Creche supervisor or caregiver should be in the loop? Tag them too. Everyone sees the same calendar.

The ZL is your first call

Anything unclear — what to check, how to handle a tough situation, whether to escalate — message your ZL on the activity's thread before the visit. Not after. You'll get an answer with the right context.

Rescheduling · No shame

Plans change. The app handles it. Just be honest.

Two ways to reschedule, depending on scale:

One activity

Open the activity card's ⋯ menu → Reschedule. Pick a new date + reason. The activity moves; your ZL sees the reason in the audit trail.

Whole creche visit

On the pitstop detail page, click Reschedule visit. Pick a new day — pitstop dates + every activity under it shift together by the same amount.

For Abdul and other RPs covering many sites: the Visit calendar drag-and-drop is the fastest way to rearrange visits week by week.

Don't fudge it

If something didn't happen, don't mark it Done just to clear the cockpit. Reschedule it honestly. The app is built around the truth — your ZL trusts it, you trust it, and you'll never be caught short in a review.
