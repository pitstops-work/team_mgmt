Module 3 · 120 minutes

Kitchen Staff — Production, Fill & Dispatch

Kitchen Cooks · helpers · loaders · kitchen manager

The kitchen is where roughly 95% of failure modes start: late food, wrong temperature, undercooked, contaminated, wrong quantity. Every container that leaves wrong, leaves wrong for eight hours. Your shift locks in the day's promise.

3.1Stores & procurement at day-start (02:00–04:00)

  • Day's menu posted on the board and matching the weekly menu calendar
  • Raw materials visible in the cook line — rice, dal, oil, vegetables, masala
  • Buffer stock for tomorrow visible in dry store — at least one day's rice, dal, and oil
  • Vegetable lot checked — no rot, no foul smell (reject criteria below)
  • Oil from a single batch, freshly opened or partly used — not visibly dark
  • Cold-chain items within use-by and stored at or below 5°C

Goods-receipt reject criteria — know these by heart

ItemReject if…
RiceMore than 2% broken, visible weevils, or off-smell
DalMore than 3% impurity or weevils
OilTurbid, off-smell, or dark colour (not freshly transparent)
VegetablesMore than 10% rot or wilt, foul smell, or water-logged
Any itemPast its visible "fresh" window — return at supplier cost

3.2Hygiene gate-walk

  • Floor washed; no standing water in the cook area
  • All cooks in apron, headcap, and closed footwear; no jewellery on hands
  • Handwash station stocked — soap, water, and drying paper or cloth
  • No mobile phones at the cook line
  • Pest-control log signed for the previous night — rat-traps, fly-screens, drain covers
  • Previous shift's garbage cleared; no overflow

3.3Filling & sealing — the lock-in moment (04:00–04:30)

For each container — roughly six per truck, twelve a day at launch:

  • Inner lining inspected — no dents, chips, or stale smell
  • Container weighed empty on the kitchen scale, witnessed
  • Filled to planned grams — units planned × 400g, plus a 5% buffer
  • Weighed full — filledKg entered in the food_app Kitchen screen
  • Lid seal tight; locking latch engaged
  • QR scanned → status FILLED (event row created)
  • Photo captured per container
  • Labelled with DP name and truck number — laminated card in the latch loop

Temperature spot-check (random 3 containers a day)

  • Thermometer probe at the container core
  • Reading ≥ 65°C at fill — otherwise back to the cook line for re-heat
  • Reading logged in the temperature log
Reject-and-replace protocol

If a container fails temperature, seal, or visible quality at fill, dispatch may be delayed by up to 20 minutes — a hard 05:20 cutoff. Beyond that, the container is dropped from the route and the affected DP is told by WhatsApp before 05:30, with the reason and tomorrow's ETA.

3.4Handing off to FILO loading (04:30–05:00)

  • Containers staged in the bay in reverse-DP order — last DP first
  • Each container scanned LOADED (FILLED → LOADED)
  • Kit items per DP confirmed against the master list and handed to the driver
  • Bay clean and dry before any container goes in

3.5Weigh-back & clean (09:30–10:15)

  • Each returned container scanned RETURNED and weighed — returnedKg entered
  • Containers washed within 30 minutes of return
  • Kit items returned and counted against load-out; losses or damage noted
  • Kitchen left clean and stores secured

3.6Kitchen Manager additions

  • Owns the 04:30 readiness gate: nothing leaves until all kits are weighed, sealed, and scanned FILLED
  • Allots the RP a covered two-wheeler parking slot during launch onboarding
  • Keeps FSSAI / FoSCoS licence visible and within validity; files procurement bills daily
  • Is the first escalation point if the kitchen is not ready by 04:30

  2. Walk through every step of filling and sealing one container, in order.
  3. A container reads 60°C at fill at 04:25. What happens, and by when must it be resolved?
