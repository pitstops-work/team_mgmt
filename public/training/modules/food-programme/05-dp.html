Module 5 · 120 minutes

Distribution Point Personnel — Setup, Serve, Close

DP 2 staff per DP · identified by Sampark

You are the face of the programme. The beneficiary never meets the cook or the driver — they meet you. Everything earlier in the chain exists so that you can hand over a hot, full, dignified plate.

5.1Setup — before the truck arrives

  • Both staff on site, in apron, headcap, and gloves
  • Serving area cleared and chosen — hard ground, not blocking the footpath, at least 5m from drains or garbage
  • Table set up level; umbrella or canopy up and actually shading the server and food
  • Today's unit cap chalked at the entry ("Today: 300 plates")
  • Queue line marked; queue-in and served-exit paths set and not crossing
  • Water cans full; cups, paper plates, dustbin, gloves, and the 400g bowl ready
  • Branded signage visible on umbrella and table-front
  • Roll-call acknowledged on the WhatsApp group by 04:00 so the kitchen knows you are operational

5.2Receiving the container

  • Help the driver offload — two people, never dragged
  • Container placed on table or clean tarp
  • Lid seal checked intact — if broken, do not serve; flag to driver and raise an exception
  • Receipt acknowledged verbally and by scanning on the DP screen

5.3Serving — the heart of the role

  • One serving point only; the server stands behind the table, no one else
  • A helper at the queue head runs "next please" and keeps the single line
  • The 400g bowl is used for every plate — never an estimate, never a different size by appearance
  • One plate per person per day; children counted on the parent's plate
  • Plate handed over with care, with both hands; beneficiaries greeted and thanked
  • Elderly, disabled, and pregnant women given priority without being made a spectacle
  • Beneficiaries leave the serve area after receiving — no clustering at the table
  • Gloves changed when visibly soiled; spills cleaned promptly

5.4When food runs short

The shortage rule

If the crowd exceeds today's cap within the first 20 minutes, announce the close-time loudly, serve the remainder, and close. Do NOT shrink the portion to stretch supply. Apologise to anyone still waiting and tell them tomorrow's time. The shortage is logged so planned units can be reviewed.

5.5Closing & handover

  • Close visibly at cap exhaustion or 60 minutes, whichever comes first
  • Headcount logged honestly — not rounded up to planned units
  • Leftovers counted honestly
  • Container resealed cleanly for the driver
  • Kit packed — table folded, umbrella down, chairs stacked, water cans rinsed, dustbin tied
  • Site swept — zero litter, no food debris, no water puddles
  • Any incident, shortage, or complaint reported to the driver and RP

5.6What is never your job

If anyone pressures you to do these, stop and tell the RP

You do not ask for ID or any proof. You do not photograph faces. You do not serve friends or family ahead of the line. You do not handle money.

Check your understanding

Three questions — answer before you move on

  1. A regular beneficiary you know asks for an extra plate for a sick neighbour. What do you do?
  2. You are 25 minutes in and the crowd is clearly larger than the cap. Walk through your actions.
  3. Why must headcount be logged honestly rather than rounded to the planned number?
