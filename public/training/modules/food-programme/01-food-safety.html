Module 1 · 75 minutes

Food Safety, Hygiene & the Cold Truth About Temperature

All roles Foundation

Food that is unsafe is worse than no food. A single food-poisoning report can pause a distribution point and damage the standing of the whole programme. This module is the floor under every other module.

1.1Personal hygiene — the baseline

  • Apron, headcap, and closed footwear worn before touching any food or container
  • No jewellery on hands; nails short and clean
  • Hands washed with soap and water; gloves worn for all food handling
  • Gloves changed the moment they are visibly soiled
  • No mobile phones at the cook line or serving point while handling food
  • Anyone with fever, cough, open cut, or stomach illness reports it and stays off the food line that day

1.2The temperature chain

Food is safe in the hot zone or the cold zone, never in between for long. The danger zone is where bacteria multiply. Our entire schedule exists to keep food out of it.

Holding-time hard cap

No served food may be more than 5 hours from time-of-cook. With dispatch at 05:00 and last serve around 08:00 we sit comfortably inside this.

Any delay that would push a serve past 09:00 means that container's serve is CANCELLED, not extended. We do not stretch the clock.

1.3Container & equipment discipline

  • Container inner lining inspected — no dents, no chipped enamel, no stale smell — before filling
  • Lid seal tight and locking latch engaged before the container leaves the kitchen
  • Seal intact when the container is offloaded at the DP — a broken seal is an exception, photographed
  • Container placed on a table or clean tarp at the DP — never directly on the ground
  • Containers washed within 30 minutes of return

1.4Water, plates, and cups

  • Drinking water from sealed cans only; cans rinsed and refilled per protocol
  • One cup per person — cups are never re-used between people
  • Paper plates kept stacked and clean; handled only with gloved hands
  • Serving point kept at least 5 metres from open drains or garbage
