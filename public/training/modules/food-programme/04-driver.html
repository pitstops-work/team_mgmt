Module 4 · 90 minutes

Truck Driver — Load, Route, Drop, Collect

Driver JustDelivery via Sampark

You are the only person who sees the food at every stop. You carry the day from the kitchen to each point and back. Your discipline on time, temperature, and handling is what keeps the chain intact between sites.

4.1FILO loading — last DP first (04:30–05:00)

Load in reverse-DP order: the last DP's container goes in first, the first DP's goes in last. Getting this wrong wastes ten minutes per stop digging out the wrong container.

  • Load order matches the printed Route Sheet (RouteStop.loadOrder)
  • Bay clean and dry before loading
  • Containers fit the layout — 6 containers (2ft dia × 3ft h), 3 folded desks, 6 water cans, 6 stools
  • Each container scanned LOADED
  • Kit-item count per DP confirmed — table, umbrella, water cans, bowls, spoons, paper plates, gloves, headcaps, cups, dustbin covers
  • Phone charged ≥ 80%, mobile data on, GPS logger ON
  • Departure checklist (laminated, in cab) signed
  • Route.dispatchedAt stamped at gate-out by 05:00

4.2On the road — route hygiene

  • Departure within 5 minutes of plan (GREEN); 5–15 min AMBER; over 15 min RED + exception
  • No stops except traffic, planned refuel, or emergency
  • No unplanned passengers in the cab
  • Drive smoothly — hard braking and cornering shift containers and spill food
  • Answer DP-staff ETA calls calmly; confirm next ETA on the WhatsApp group
  • GPS dot visibly moving on the live map

4.3At each DP — the Drop

  • Arrival stamped — scan DELIVERED
  • DP staff already on site with a cleared serving area
  • Container offloaded by two people — you and DP staff — never dragged
  • Container placed on table or clean tarp, never on the ground
  • Lid seal intact — a broken seal is an exception, photographed
  • droppedKg, GPS, and drop photo captured
  • DP staff acknowledges receipt verbally and by scanning on the DP screen
When the DP is a shelter

At a shelter you serve personally rather than dropping and leaving. Everywhere else you do not stay to serve — the DP team serves, you move to the next drop.

4.4Between drops

  • Kit items for a served DP stay there for the pickup loop — they do not go back in the truck
  • No unplanned route deviation

4.5At each DP — the Collect (45–60 min after drop)

  • Arrive in the window middle, around 50 minutes after drop
  • Service has ended before pickup — no plates being served when you arrive
  • Lid closed; leftovers visible and estimated
  • pickupAt, pickup photo, and headcountServed captured
  • Kit packed — table folded, umbrella down, chairs stacked, water cans rinsed, dustbin tied
  • DP area swept of plates, debris, and spills — no trace left behind
  • Container loaded back FIFO (first dropped, first picked up) and scanned PICKED_UP

4.6Return to kitchen

  • Route.arrivedAt stamped on arrival (~10:00)
  • Containers handed for weigh-back; kit items returned for counting
Red flags — stop and raise an exception with photo immediately

Broken seal en route · container dropped or spilled · DP staff absent at drop · service still ongoing at pickup · area not cleaned at pickup · any fight or incident at a DP.

Vehicle breakdown: call your JustDelivery line, the RP, and the ZL immediately. JustDelivery is responsible for driver and truck replacement during operations.

Check your understanding

Three questions — answer before you move on

  1. Your route is DP1 → DP2 → DP3. In what order do the containers go into the bay, and why?
  2. You reach a DP for pickup and people are still being served. What is this, and what do you do?
  3. The seal on a container is broken when you arrive at DP2. List your steps.
