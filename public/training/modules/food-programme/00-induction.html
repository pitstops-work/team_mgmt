Module 0 · 60 minutes

Induction — The Programme and the Promise

All roles Foundation · run this for the whole intake together

Everyone in this programme is handling food that someone will depend on for the day. The chain is only as strong as its weakest link: food cooked correctly but served with contempt fails; food served with dignity but cold or late fails. This module sets the floor.

0.1What this programme is

We serve a hot, dignified meal to people in labour addas, shelters, and settlements across Bangalore. At launch that is roughly 1,500 meals a day from one kitchen across five distribution points; the design scales to roughly 15,000–20,000 meals a day across about sixty points. The food is produced by a certified vendor, moved by a small truck fleet, and served at fixed points by trained personnel.

0.2The chain — and where you sit in it

Learn this sequence. It is the spine of everything that follows.

  • Cook — the kitchen produces and fills containers to weight and temperature.
  • Fill & seal — each container is weighed, sealed, QR-scanned FILLED, and photographed.
  • Load (FILO) — the truck is loaded last-DP-first so the first stop comes off first.
  • Drop — the truck delivers a sealed container to each DP; scanned DELIVERED.
  • Serve — DP staff serve one 400g portion per person, with dignity, in a managed queue.
  • Collect — the truck returns 45–60 minutes later, packs the kit, leaves no trace; scanned PICKED_UP.
  • Reconcile — the kitchen weighs back; wastage and headcount are recorded; the day is closed.

0.4The non-negotiables — every role, every day

Hard rules

One plate per person per day. No re-serves. No exceptions.

The full 400g bowl is used to serve — never an estimate by hand, never a different size by appearance.

No ID, ration card, or proof is ever asked. The programme is unconditional.

No photography of beneficiary faces, ever.

Food is served, not dumped or thrown. Beneficiaries are guests, not subjects.

No money changes hands at any point in the field chain.

The one rule above all others

We own this programme. Vendor risk sits with Sampark contractually, but quality risk sits with us. "The vendor was late" is never an answer — noticing it, escalating it, and writing down what we learn is the job.

Status language — used everywhere

Train these three words until they are reflex. They appear on every checklist and every report.

GREEN on plan, no action AMBER drifting, watch and note RED stop, fix, or escalate now
