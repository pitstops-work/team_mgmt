Module 9

Trainer's Guide

RP ZL For anyone delivering this curriculum

9.1Sequencing

Run the three foundation modules (0–2) for the whole intake together, regardless of role — it builds a shared language and lets people see the chain they sit inside. Then split into role modules. Keep cohorts small enough that everyone gets hands on a container, a bowl, and the food_app screen.

9.2Method — show, do, sign

  • Show — the trainer demonstrates the real task at the real site at the real time (a 02:00 kitchen audit is taught at 02:00, not in a classroom)
  • Do — the trainee performs it under supervision until it is clean
  • Sign — the RP records the sign-off only after a clean supervised run, using the Module 7 record

9.3Where to spend the most time

RoleThe 20% that causes 80% of failures
KitchenTemperature at fill and the seal. Drill the reject-and-replace clock until it is reflex.
DriverFILO order and the 45–60 min pickup window. Most lost time and cold food trace here.
DPThe 400g bowl and the shortage rule. These are where dignity is won or lost in public.
RPThe reconciliation formula and escalation judgement — knowing what is a phone call vs an Exception.

9.4Hosting in the Pitstops Training Hub

This module is written to drop into the Pitstops Training Hub. Suggested structure:

  • One Training Hub entry per module, tagged by role, so a new DP hire sees only Modules 0–2 and 5
  • The check-your-understanding questions become the written-assessment item bank
  • The Module 7 sign-off record maps to a per-trainee completion gate, signed by the RP
  • The Module 8 cards export as printable assets under public/ (the SOP already proposes a Dignity Charter card)
  • Recurrence and the live checklists stay in food_app and the existing Pitstops daily-gate template — this module is the teaching layer, not a second system of record. Avoid double-bookkeeping.
