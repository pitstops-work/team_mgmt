2.1The dignity charter

Beneficiaries are guests, not subjects. The behaviours below are never acceptable. Anyone who sees them corrects on the spot; the RP retrains the same day and reports.

Never acceptableWhy it breaks dignity
Raising your voice at or scolding a beneficiaryPublic shaming
Throwing or dropping the plate for the person to pick upTreats food as charity, not service
Photographing beneficiary facesConsent violation; can become evidence against them
Asking for ID, ration card, or any proofThe programme is unconditional
Conditional serving ("only if you stand here")Conditional charity
Serving friends or family ahead of the queueFavouritism
Different portions by appearanceDiscrimination — use the 400g bowl every time
Visible disgust at a beneficiaryDemeaning
What we also want to see — and log

The server greets people. The server thanks people at handover. Staff help the elderly, disabled, and pregnant women without being asked, and without making a spectacle of it. Staff clean without being told. We learn from these too.

2.2Crowd management — the Peenya lesson

One lesson from the pilot governs every queue: a fixed unit cap plus a visible queue creates order; ambiguity creates chaos.

Before serving begins

  • Today's unit cap chalked or signed at the entry ("Today: 300 plates")
  • Queue line marked with rope, chalk, or cones
  • Two clear paths — queue-in and served-exit — that do not cross
  • Only the server stands behind the table; a helper at the queue head manages "next please"

During and at cut-off

  • One plate per person; children counted on the parent's plate, not their own place in line
  • Disputes — the server pauses until the queue resolves; no shouting
  • If the crowd exceeds planned units in the first 20 minutes, announce the close-time loudly, serve the remainder, and close
Never stretch supply by shrinking the plate

When food runs short you announce and close — you do NOT reduce portion size to stretch it. Shrinking the portion to serve more people breaks the dignity charter. Apologise to anyone still in the queue and tell them tomorrow's time. Log the shortage.

2.3food_app — what everyone needs to know

food_app is the single source of operational truth. Each role uses its own screen, but the data joins into one record per container per day.

Role screenWhat you do on it
KitchenEnter filledKg, scan FILLED, photograph each container, stamp dispatch
DriverScan LOADED → DELIVERED → PICKED_UP → RETURNED; capture GPS, photos, droppedKg
DPAcknowledge receipt, log headcountServed and leftovers honestly
Manager / RPLive map, reconciliation, raise and resolve Exceptions (with audio)

The container moves through five statuses: FILLED → LOADED → DELIVERED → PICKED_UP → RETURNED. Every scan is timestamped. If you skip a scan, the day's reconciliation cannot close — so scanning is not paperwork, it is the operation.

Exceptions — the universal tool

Anything wrong — a broken seal, a late truck, an absent DP staffer, a shortage, an incident — is raised as an Exception in food_app, with a photo and a 30–90 second voice note. The voice note is timestamped, so record it before food leaves or before you move on. When in doubt, raise it.
