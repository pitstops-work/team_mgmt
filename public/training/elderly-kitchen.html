Elderly Community Kitchen

PITSTOP 1 OF 6 · RESEARCH · 15 DAYS

Kitchen Identification

Why this pitstop

Identify 3 kitchen woman/women from the community. Each kitchen serves 15 elderly for lunch daily (counts for lunch and dinner). The kitchen woman must stay home, cook reliably every day, and be trusted by the community. She is paid ₹3,000/month. CO shortlists candidates; RP visits to confirm final selection.

1.
Confirm number of kitchens needed based on needs assessment data for this settlement
2.
CO identifies eligible women in community (stays home, cooks regularly, trusted)
3.
Verify each candidate: cooks daily at home, not employed outside, physically capable
4.
Verify community standing and trust for each candidate
5.
CO prepares shortlist and presents to RP
6.
RP visits to confirm final selection for each kitchen
7.
Formalise understanding with selected kitchen women (role, stipend, daily expectations)
8.
Document kitchen locations and addresses
PITSTOP 2 OF 6 · BUDGETING · 20 DAYS

Procurement Setup

Why this pitstop

Set up all supply chains and infrastructure for 3 kitchen(s). Grocery is procured monthly and stored at the resource centre; CO rations stock to each kitchen. Vegetables are procured every 3 days by the CO from a vetted vendor — payment is handled by partner accounts, not the CO. Kitchen equipment is provided only where something is missing.

1.
Identify and vet monthly grocery vendor (rice, dal, oil, jaggery, eggs, ragi)
2.
Identify and vet vegetable vendor for 3-day cycle supply
3.
Confirm vegetable vendor payment process with partner accounts team (not through CO)
4.
Set up gas connection for each kitchen (or verify existing connection is functional)
5.
Assess and procure missing kitchen equipment: stove, vessels, plates
6.
Procure lunchboxes for home delivery to bed-ridden elderly
7.
Set up storage space in resource centre for monthly grocery stock
8.
Set up inventory register and usage tracker for each kitchen (pen and paper)
9.
Place first month grocery order and confirm delivery schedule
10.
RP visits to confirm procurement setup is complete
PITSTOP 3 OF 6 · TRAINING · 30 DAYS

Training

Why this pitstop

RP conducts cluster-level training for all COs covering hygiene, inventory management, menu, and delivery procedures. COs then deliver this training to kitchen women. Menu is fixed: rice + one vegetable side dish + ragi mudde + one boiled egg per person, served at lunch.

1.
RP conducts cluster-level training for all COs (hygiene, inventory management, menu)
2.
CO trains kitchen woman on food hygiene: handwashing, storage, utensil cleaning
3.
CO trains kitchen woman on the fixed menu: rice + vegetable side dish + ragi mudde + boiled egg
4.
CO trains kitchen woman on correct portion sizes for 15 persons
5.
CO trains kitchen woman on inventory register maintenance
6.
CO explains vegetable delivery process (CO delivers every 3 days; kitchen woman does not procure)
7.
CO demonstrates lunchbox packing and delivery procedure for bed-ridden elderly
8.
Conduct mock cook or trial run at each kitchen
9.
Kitchen woman confirms she understands escalation process (CO is first contact)
PITSTOP 4 OF 6 · MEETING · 32 DAYS

Enrollment

Why this pitstop

CO enrolls 15 elderly per kitchen. The list is largely prepared from prior community assessment. CO visits each elderly person at home, explains the programme, and notes any special needs. Bed-ridden elderly requiring home delivery are flagged.

1.
CO finalises list of 15 elderly per kitchen from prior assessment
2.
CO visits each enrolled elderly to explain the programme
3.
Explain kitchen location, timings, and how to reach
4.
Explain grievance redressal mechanism (contact CO directly)
5.
Identify any special nutritional needs based on health condition
6.
Flag bed-ridden elderly requiring daily home delivery and note addresses
7.
Document enrollment details for all 15 per kitchen
8.
RP verifies enrollment — meets a sample of enrolled elderly
PITSTOP 5 OF 6 · MEETING · 35 DAYS

Rollout

Why this pitstop

Start kitchen operations. CO is present for the first few days to mobilise elderly and ensure smooth running. Track wastage, collect taste feedback, and confirm home delivery to bed-ridden is working.

1.
Kitchen starts on agreed date
2.
CO present on Day 1 to mobilise enrolled elderly and ensure turnout
3.
CO present for first 3–5 days until operations are stable
4.
Food wastage tracked and portions adjusted if needed
5.
Taste and quality feedback collected from elderly
6.
Home delivery to bed-ridden elderly activated and confirmed working
7.
First vegetable delivery received and quality verified (3-day cycle)
8.
Any issues recorded in register and escalated to RP
9.
RP visits during rollout phase to observe and support
PITSTOP 6 OF 6 · REVIEW · 65 DAYS

Monitoring & Tracking

Why this pitstop

Monthly monitoring of all 3 community kitchen(s). CO visits daily; RP visits all kitchens in the cluster monthly. Covers food quality, inventory, beneficiary coverage, vendor management, and substitute arrangements. Elderly kitchens are also a standing agenda item in the monthly cluster meeting.

1.
CO daily visit log reviewed for all kitchens
2.
Random food quality check conducted (taste, quantity, hygiene)
3.
Feedback collected from enrolled elderly on food quality and satisfaction
4.
Verify all enrolled elderly are receiving elderly pension
5.
Identify elderly with additional needs and refer to elderly programme team
6.
Inventory register checked — stock matched against usage
7.
Vegetable vendor delivery and quality verified (3-day cycle)
8.
Check for leakage — overpayment, diversion, or missing stock
9.
Vendor performance reviewed; escalate or change vendor if needed
10.
Substitute kitchen arrangement confirmed for when kitchen woman is on leave
11.
RP monthly visit to all kitchens in cluster completed
12.
Elderly kitchens reviewed as agenda item in monthly cluster meeting
