PITSTOP 1 OF 10 · MILESTONE · 21 DAYS

Team Recruitment & Deployment

Why this pitstop

Recruit staff for 2 Children Learning Centre(s) — 3 staff per centre. Required: 2 Centre Coordinator(s) (graduate, preferably from community), 4 Children Outreach Worker(s) (from community), 1 Children Lead (mandatory when >1 centre). For expansion: coordinator must be a graduate; outreach workers must be from community; no prior experience required — interest in working with children is essential.

1.
Recruit Children Lead (1 per organisation, required for >1 centre)
✓Children Lead Recruitment
2.
Recruit 2 Centre Coordinator(s) — graduate, preferably from community
✓Centre Coordinator Recruitment
3.
Recruit 4 Children Outreach Worker(s) — must be from community
✓Outreach Worker Recruitment
4.
Review and map any existing staff from current programme who are interested and equipped for children work
✓Existing Staff Capability Review
5.
Conduct team induction: programme objectives, child rights, safe space principles
🎙Team Induction Session
6.
Assign staff to centres and clusters
✓Staff Centre Assignment
7.
Set up team communication channels and weekly review schedule
✓Team Communication Setup
PITSTOP 2 OF 10 · SITE VISIT · 28 DAYS

Centre Location Identification & Rent Agreement

Why this pitstop

Identify suitable location(s) for 2 Children Learning Centre(s). Each CLC must be within 1–1.5 km radius of the target community. Minimum 400–500 sqft for learning corners, peer discussions, and group activities, plus outdoor play space. Basic facilities — safe drinking water and functional toilets — are non-negotiable.

1.
Shortlist 2–3 potential buildings per cluster (within 1–1.5 km radius of community)
🎙Centre Location Site Visit
2.
Verify minimum 400–500 sqft indoor space for learning corners and group activities
✓Space Requirements Verification
3.
Verify safe drinking water supply
✓Water Supply Verification
4.
Verify functional and usable toilets
✓Toilet Functionality Check
5.
Check for outdoor/play space adjacent to or near the building
✓Outdoor Space Assessment
6.
Assess ventilation, natural lighting, and basic structural safety
✓Building Safety Assessment
7.
Confirm with cluster coordinator and children lead
✓Location Approval Meeting
8.
Execute rent agreement(s) for 2 centre location(s)
📎Centre Rent Agreement Signing

Books, TLM & AV Materials Procurement

Low-cost and no-cost materials are prioritised. Establish a human resource bank of subject experts and resource persons for co-facilitation. Secure DI (District Institute, Bangalore & Ramanagara) support for capacity building.

Procure art & craft supplies, indoor games, and board games

Procure sports equipment for outdoor and indoor play

PITSTOP 5 OF 10 · RESEARCH · 55 DAYS

Children Survey & Baseline Learning Assessment

Why this pitstop

Outreach workers conduct door-to-door survey of all children aged 4–14 within 1–1.5 km of each CLC. Identify enrolment status, dropouts, and non-enrolled children. For each child enrolled at the CLC, conduct a friendly informal baseline assessment (oral, worksheet, game-based) — not to grade them, but to help the coordinator plan tailored support. Conducted with DI support.

1.
Outreach workers conduct door-to-door survey of children aged 4–14 in cluster
🎙Children Baseline Survey
2.
Record per child: name, gender, DOB, school/anganwadi status, mother tongue, health concerns
✓Child Profile Data Entry
3.
Identify children with irregular school attendance
✓Irregular Attendance Identification
4.
Identify children who have dropped out entirely
✓Dropout Children Identification
5.
Identify children never enrolled in school or anganwadi
✓Never-Enrolled Children Identification
6.
Conduct baseline learning assessment per child (with DI support) — informal, game-based, not graded
✓Child Baseline Learning Assessment
7.
Assess language: letter recognition, reading words/sentences, listening comprehension
✓Language Proficiency Assessment
8.
Assess maths: number recognition, basic operations, word problems
✓Numeracy Proficiency Assessment
9.
Assess general awareness for younger children: body parts, colours, surroundings
✓Young Child Awareness Assessment
10.
Enrol first cohort of 200 children across 2 centre(s) — enter profiles in MIS
✓Child MIS Enrollment
11.
Prepare per-child activity plan based on assessment findings
✓Individual Activity Plan Preparation
PITSTOP 6 OF 10 · TRAINING · 50 DAYS

Staff Capacity Building Programme

Why this pitstop

All Centre Coordinators and Outreach Workers undergo 2 days of training per month throughout Year 1 (calendarized). First module covers: safe space facilitation, child development, reading and writing support methods, baseline assessment tools, library circles, and MIS app. DI resource persons support monthly. External resource persons engaged as needed.

1.
Conduct initial 2-day orientation: programme objectives, child rights, safe space principles
🎙Staff Capacity Building Orientation
2.
Train on age-appropriate learning support: reading, writing, numeracy — informal methods
🎙Learning Support Methods Training
3.
Train on baseline assessment tools (observation, oral, worksheet, game-based)
🎙Assessment Tool Training
4.
Train on facilitating library circles and promoting reading habits
🎙Library Circle Facilitation Training
5.
Train on life skills and socio-emotional learning facilitation
🎙Life Skills Facilitation Training
6.
Train on using AV materials, low-cost and no-cost teaching aids
🎙Teaching Aids and AV Training
7.
Train on MIS app: child profiling, attendance tracking, reading/writing progress, library usage
🎙MIS App Training
8.
Finalise 12-month monthly training calendar with DI support
✓Annual Training Calendar Finalisation
9.
Identify and onboard external resource persons for co-facilitation (life skills, digital, sports)
✓External Resource Person Onboarding
PITSTOP 7 OF 10 · MEETING · 65 DAYS

Centre Operations Launch

Why this pitstop

Launch all 2 CLC(s) and begin daily operations. Daily schedule: 3:30 pm onwards — safe space + snack for Anganwadi returnees (4–6 yrs); 4:30–6 pm — safe space, homework support, reading/writing for school children (7–14 yrs); 6–7 pm — library circles, movie/documentary screening, art & craft, indoor/outdoor sports, singing and music. Snacks (egg/chana/banana cooked by rotating mothers) for 50 children per day per centre.

1.
Confirm facility readiness: space, materials, staff, water, toilets
✓Pre-Launch Facility Readiness Check
2.
Conduct community meeting to announce CLC launch and daily schedule
✓CLC Launch Community Meeting
3.
Set up snack programme — identify rotating mothers to cook (egg/chana/banana for 50 children/day)
✓Snack Programme Setup
4.
Launch 3:30 pm slot: safe space and snack for Anganwadi returnees (4–6 yrs)
✓Anganwadi Returnees Slot Launch
5.
Launch 4:30–6 pm slot: safe space, homework support, reading/writing for school children
✓School Children Slot Launch
6.
Launch 6–7 pm slot: library circles, art & craft, music, indoor/outdoor sports, movie screenings
✓Evening Enrichment Slot Launch
7.
Begin child attendance tracking in MIS from Day 1 (CLC and school attendance)
✓Child Attendance Tracking Setup
8.
Prepare activity plan and weekly schedule (themed sessions, sports, creative activities)
✓Weekly Activity Plan Preparation
9.
Confirm all 200 children enrolled and profiled in MIS across 2 centre(s)
✓MIS Enrollment Verification
PITSTOP 8 OF 10 · MILESTONE · 90 DAYS

School & Anganwadi Linkage + Re-enrolment Drive

Why this pitstop

Outreach workers run a continuous school and anganwadi linkage programme. Age 4–6: focus on anganwadi enrolment. Age 7–11: maintain school attendance, support with irregular attendees. Age 12–14: targeted re-enrolment and referrals. Outreach workers visit schools monthly, meet teachers, track irregular children, visit anganwadis, and follow up with parents.

1.
Outreach workers visit all govt schools in cluster — meet teachers, collect data on irregular/dropout children
🎙School & Anganwadi Outreach Visit
2.
Visit anganwadis — meet AWW, identify 4–6 yr children not yet enrolled
🎙Anganwadi Coordination Visit
3.
Visit parents of identified dropout, irregular, and non-enrolled children
🎙Parent Follow-up Home Visit
4.
Facilitate enrolment of 4–6 yr olds in anganwadi
✓Anganwadi Enrollment Support
5.
Facilitate re-enrolment of 7–11 yr dropouts in school
✓School Re-enrollment Support
6.
Engage dropout children at CLC through art, craft, indoor games, and conversations
✓Dropout Re-engagement Session
7.
Refer 12–14 yr dropouts to bridge courses, open schooling, or vocational options
✓Dropout Pathway Counselling
8.
Launch back-to-school campaign in community
✓Back-to-School Community Campaign
9.
Map scholarship entitlements for eligible children (post-matric, minority, SC/ST)
✓Scholarship Entitlement Mapping
10.
Track re-enrolment outcomes and school attendance per child in MIS
✓Re-enrollment Outcome Tracking
