WHY THIS GOAL MATTERS

Our work runs through partners. Bad partners cost more than no partners.

The partner is the operational arm we don't directly manage. The relationship is the only thing standing between funder expectations and field reality.

Due diligence first

Financial · governance · field track record · references · before signing any MoU. Partner failures usually trace to skipped DD.

MoU not handshake

Written deliverables · milestone payments · data sharing · escalation path · exit clauses. Verbal agreements end in disputes.

Quarterly joint review

Both sides at the table · same data view · same outcomes scorecard · one set of action items · no parallel narratives

Annual health check

Once a year · governance + financial + programmatic + relationship temperature · written report · informs renewal decision

PITSTOP 1 OF 6 · RESEARCH · 21 DAYS

Partner Mapping & Due Diligence

Why this pitstop

Identify and vet 2 prospective partner(s). Due diligence is non-negotiable — a bad partnership costs more than no partnership.

1.
Longlist of 4+ potential partners identified
2.
Shortlisted to 2 based on geographic fit, credibility, and track record
3.
Online presence, annual reports, and references reviewed for each
4.
Informal reference checks done (2+ per partner)
5.
Each partner's alignment with our programme approach confirmed
6.
Due diligence summary shared with manager
7.
Approval to proceed with outreach obtained
PITSTOP 2 OF 6 · MEETING · 60 DAYS

Partnership Scoping & MOU Negotiation

Why this pitstop

Negotiate the terms of partnership with each new partner. Be specific about roles, deliverables, data sharing, financial accountability, and exit terms in the MOU.

1.
Partnership scoping meeting held with each partner
2.
Roles and responsibilities clearly agreed (who does what, where)
3.
Deliverables and timelines agreed
4.
Financial accountability terms (if funds involved) negotiated
5.
Data sharing and consent protocols agreed
6.
MOU draft reviewed by Director and legal
7.
MOU signed by both parties
8.
Signed MOU filed and shared with finance and programme team
PITSTOP 3 OF 6 · TRAINING · 90 DAYS

Partner Onboarding & Joint Planning

Why this pitstop

Orient each new partner to our programme approach, tools, and quality standards. Co-create the joint implementation plan for the first quarter.

1.
Partner team introduced to our programme framework
2.
Data collection tools and MIS walkthrough completed
3.
Quality standards and non-negotiables communicated
4.
Field visit to partner's operational area completed
5.
First-quarter joint work plan co-created
6.
Primary points of contact confirmed on both sides
7.
Communication and escalation protocol agreed
PITSTOP 4 OF 6 · MEETING · 180 DAYS

Quarterly Partner Joint Review

Why this pitstop

Quarterly review with all 2 partner(s). Review joint deliverables, address friction, and plan the next quarter. Be honest about what is and isn't working.

1.
All 2 partner(s) present at review
2.
Previous quarter deliverables reviewed against plan
3.
Data quality and reporting timeliness discussed
4.
Financial utilisation reviewed (if funds are involved)
5.
Friction points surfaced and resolved or escalated
6.
Next quarter joint plan confirmed
7.
Minutes documented and shared within 5 days
PITSTOP 5 OF 6 · REVIEW · 175 DAYS

Partner Deliverable & Reporting Audit

Why this pitstop

Quality audit of what partners have actually delivered vs. committed. Don't conflate activity (meetings held, visits done) with outcome (children enrolled, cases resolved).

1.
Deliverable log updated for all 2 partner(s)
2.
Commitments vs. actuals documented
3.
Evidence of key deliverables collected (reports, photos, beneficiary data)
4.
Variance between committed and delivered noted with explanation
5.
Financial utilisation vs. milestones verified
6.
Corrective actions agreed where needed
7.
Audit summary shared with manager
PITSTOP 6 OF 6 · REVIEW · 365 DAYS

Annual Partnership Health Review

Why this pitstop

Annual 360° review of each partnership. Is it working? Is the partner delivering? Is the relationship healthy? This informs renewal, scale-up, or exit decisions.

1.
Annual outcome data compiled for all 2 partner(s)
2.
Health check conversation held with each partner (frank, two-way)
3.
Partner satisfaction with the relationship assessed
4.
Our satisfaction with the partner's delivery assessed
5.
Renewal, scale-up, or exit recommendation drafted per partner
6.
Recommendation shared with Director
7.
Decision communicated to partner with adequate notice
