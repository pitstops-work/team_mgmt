PRIORITY 1 · CRECHES

Saturate. Don't dilute.

A simple rule: where we have a creche programme, every eligible child should be enrolled before we move to the next geography.

What "saturation" means
In a settlement with 110 households and ~45 children 0–3 years, a saturated programme has ≥ 38 of them enrolled, attending ≥ 60% days, and with growth-monitoring on schedule. Below that — we are not done.
What "dilution" looks like
Opening a new creche in a new settlement while the original one is at 23 of 45 children. We will not do this in 2026.
RPs — your job this year
Audit your current creches. Honest enrolment number. Honest attendance. If you're below saturation — that gap is your first set of pitstops, not the next new creche.
PRIORITY 2 · CHILDREN'S WORK

Expand into the next age bracket.

The 4–14 year cohort: learning centres, after-school, school-transition. We have a head start; this year we go deeper.

Where it goes well
Children's learning centres anchored in settlements with active creches. Continuity from 3-year-olds → 6-year-olds → 10-year-olds. Same team, same trust, lower drop-off.
Where it stumbles
Standalone learning centres in settlements where we have no prior relationship. Recruitment is hard, attendance fragile. We will not start these.
PRIORITY 3 · ELDERLY + KITCHENS

Expand both. The kitchen becomes the everyday anchor.

Elderly centres alone are too thin. Without the kitchen, the centre becomes a once-a-week health camp. With the kitchen, the centre becomes the place a person comes every day. Daily contact is the unit of dignity.
A kitchen is a logistics operation. Daily cooking, daily delivery, daily count, daily quality. We will be deeply unromantic about it. The metrics that matter: cost per meal, meals per day, days per month, complaint rate.
Target this year: kitchens running 25 op days/month at ≤ ₹30/meal blended cost in cities where we operate. ZL has the ownership; RP runs the daily ops; PM holds the cost line.
PRIORITY 4 · YOUTH

Stabilise. This is not the year to scale.

The hardest priority to internalise — because youth work feels exciting and donors love it.

Three existing youth resource centres are below the rigour bar. Attendance dips. Activities feel performative. Some "youth groups" have not met in weeks. We will fix these — before we open a fourth.
"Stabilise" means: real attendance, real curriculum, real outcomes (vocational referrals, education continuity, civic engagement). Less performative. More honest.
If by mid-year we cannot stabilise a centre, we close it. Closing is also an outcome.
PRIORITY 5 · FOOD

Expand — carefully. The model is alive.

Two more distribution points this year per zone where load justifies it. Vendor diversification (no zone runs on a single vendor). DP-team training: every new DP gets a 21-day pitstop arc before launch.
Cost discipline is the binding constraint. ₹/meal goes up the moment we get sloppy — on ingredient sourcing, on transport, on staffing ratios. We will publish ₹/meal monthly to the team.
CFAR-Sampark-PST comparison stays live: as we expand, we keep choosing between routes per geography rather than locking in one route everywhere.
PRIORITY 6 · TOILET + WATER

Figure-out year. Not a roll-out year.

We do not yet know if we are the right ones to do community toilets and drinking water at scale. This year we find out.

One pilot toilet complex per city
Site selection, design, operate, maintain. With one partner each. End-of-year evaluation against three things: usage, cleanliness, financial sustainability of the o-and-m model.
Drinking water — desk-research phase first
Map existing efforts (water ATMs, RO plants, community storage). Understand who is doing this well. Only after that — possibly one pilot site.
We are explicitly fine concluding "this isn't our work"
If at end-of-year the answer is "we should hand this back to the State or a specialised CSO" — that is an acceptable outcome. Better than pretending we can.
PRIORITY 7 · CIVIC AMENITIES

From "welfare-rights" to a real civic-amenities programme.

Settlement infrastructure — roads, drains, streetlights, garbage, water connections — is core work now, not a side activity.

Baseline mapping in every settlement. Working drains? Functional lights? Garbage frequency? Water connection coverage? Numbers, not impressions.
Demand-letter cycle. Quarterly. To ward councillor / GHMC / GCC / BBMP. Tracked, escalated, followed up. Not a one-off campaign.
Community leader bench. 2 to 3 leaders per settlement who can carry the demand independently of us. The whole point is that this continues when we step back.
PRIORITIES 8 + 9 + 10 · SEEDING · GEOGRAPHY · NEW NEEDS

The slower, longer-arc work.

8 · Seed new partners
Two to three small CSOs we have been observing — begin a structured engagement. Small operational grant. Heavy co-walking. By end of year, decide if any becomes a Type-2 or Type-3 relationship.
9 · Geographic expansion
Two new zones — one in each city. Selection criteria: high slum density, low NGO presence, no overlap with our existing zones, partner-gap that we can fill. PM proposes; we collectively decide by Q1.
10 · New real needs
A standing quarterly slot at zone review for "what came up this quarter that isn't on our annual plan". No need is too small to surface. Some will turn into next year's priorities.
HOW THIS BECOMES YOUR WORK

From this slide to your pitstops.

Each priority becomes one or more goals on your account. Each goal becomes a sequence of pitstops with dates. That's the bridge.

RP
3 to 6 goals on your account for the year. Most from this list. Each goal carries a pitstop arc with monthly milestones.
ZL
Aggregate goal across your zone — saturation numbers, kitchen ops, civic-amenities cycle. Your goals are the union of your RPs' goals plus the zone-level commitments.
PM
Cross-zone goals: geographic expansion, new partner seeding, cost discipline, MIS rigour. PM goals are mostly about supervision, not direct execution.

Right after lunch — we open pitstops and build these. ZL and PM walk between tables. By 5:30 today, every person in this room has their year on their phone.

QUESTIONS

Question the plan now. Not in March.

If a priority feels off — push back. If something is missing — say it. The goal-building session that follows lock the year.

"Saturation before expansion — what if a current geography genuinely can't reach saturation this year?"
"Stabilising youth means closing weak centres — how do we communicate that to the youth themselves?"
"The toilet/water 'figure-out' year — what if we discover halfway it's clearly working?"
"Two new zones in two cities is ambitious. Where's the bandwidth coming from?"
"Is there a priority missing from this list that you've been quietly thinking about?"

Open your phones. Open pitstops. Let's build.
