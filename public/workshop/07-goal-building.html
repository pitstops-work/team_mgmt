SETUP · 10 MIN

Get the basics in place before we start.

Every team member does this first. ZL and PM will walk around to help.

1.
Open pitstops on your phone
https://pitstops.work · sign in with your Google account. If you don't have access, raise your hand.
2.
Confirm your role
Top right corner of the home screen. RP / ZL / PM / Leader. If wrong — flag to PM, they can fix on the spot.
3.
Open the training hub bookmark
/pitstops-training.html — for any goal template you are unsure about, the deck is one tap away. Use it.
4.
Have the ops-plan deck open in another tab
The ten priorities are your menu. Every goal you create should map back to one of those priorities — or be a "new real need" you can defend.
IF YOU ARE AN RP

Build 3 to 6 goals — your year on the ground.

Don't optimise for impressive count. Optimise for what you can actually run with rigour.

For each priority you own this year
Pick the most appropriate goal template from the hub. Fill in the parameters (number of clusters, settlements, beneficiaries). Save. The pitstops will auto-generate.
Then sequence them
Stagger start dates across the year. Don't put six goals all starting January 1. Look at the heat-map of your team's capacity. Be honest about what gets done in Q1 versus Q3.
Add the saturation goals first
If you have current creches below saturation — those are your first goals. Don't skip ahead to the shiny new things.
Realism check before locking
Look at the total pitstops across all your goals. If it is over ~70 for the year, you are over-promising. Cut back. Better to commit to less and over-deliver than to lose the year by Q2.
IF YOU ARE A ZL

Aggregate. Review. Lock cluster-level commitments.

First — sit with each of your RPs
Walk through what they have drafted. Ask: is this realistic? What did they forget? What did they over-stretch on? Sign off before they lock it.
Then — build your zone-level goals
Civic-amenities baseline across your cluster. Community-leader bench numbers. Kitchen-ops oversight. Welfare-rights cycle. These are your goals — not aggregations of RP goals.
Watch for gaps
If your zone has 14 settlements and your RPs' goals only cover 9 of them — that's a problem to surface, not paper over. Either someone takes more, or the zone is deliberately leaving 5 settlements unattended (and we should say so).
IF YOU ARE A PM

Supervisor's lens — cross-zone, cross-priority, cross-cost.

Goals you own personally
Geographic expansion (one new zone in your city). New partner seeding. Cost discipline across all programmes. Quarterly "new real needs" review setup. Internal MIS rigour.
Goals you ratify, not create
Each ZL's zone-level commitments. Walk through them. Push back on anything that smells like wishful thinking. Approve only what you would defend in a year-end review.
Watch the ratios
Total pitstops per RP. Total active goals per zone. Cost-per-meal target for each kitchen. If any number stretches beyond the historical pattern — interrogate it before letting it lock.
WHAT "DONE" LOOKS LIKE

By 5:30 today — every person in this room has these.

All goals for the year created and saved on your pitstops account
Each goal has the right needs-domain, the right settlement / cluster scope, and the right parameters
Pitstop dates are sequenced across the calendar — not all bunched in one quarter
Your ZL has reviewed and signed off (RPs)
Your PM has reviewed and signed off (ZLs)
Anything left unresolved is written on the workshop whiteboard — we don't take it home in your head

If you finish early — help the person next to you. The workshop ends when everyone is done. Not when the clock says 5:30.

THE NEXT 7 DAYS

What you take home — and what we do next.

Two days of conversation. The real test starts when you are alone with your settlement.

Within 48 hours — sanity check on the field
Visit at least one settlement covered by one of your new goals. Walk it again. See if anything you committed in the room makes less sense once you are back on the ground. If so — edit. The plan is a draft until it survives the field.
Within 7 days — partner conversations
For every goal involving a partner — schedule a sit-down. Walk them through what you are committing to. Listen to their version of the constraints. Update the goal if their inputs change your thinking.
Within 14 days — first pitstop review
The first set of pitstops starts firing. ZL reviews them in zone meetings. PM watches the dashboard. If something is already slipping in week 2 — that's the year telling us something. Pay attention.
WORKSHOP CLOSE · 30 MIN OPEN Q&A

Ask anything. Push on anything. This is the last slot.

Things from yesterday that still don't sit right. Things you would have said but didn't. Things you would be afraid to ask over email.

What did we get wrong this year that we are not yet admitting?
What is one thing about our stance that you privately disagree with?
What is the one habit, of yours or the team's, that you most want to change in 2026?
What is the one farce in our own work we should call out this year?
Anything else. No saving it for a 1:1.
