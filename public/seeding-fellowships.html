Why this programme

Three things exist. They rarely meet.

Civil society needs young, capable, committed people willing to build in under-served geographies. The problem is a coordination failure, not a talent shortage.

🎯
WHAT
Needs to be done
Clear priority themes: livelihoods, health, children, crèches, sanitation, welfare rights.
✕
🧑‍🎓
WHO
Can do it
~70% of India under 40. Trained social-work / development graduates. Alumni looking for mission.
✕
📍
WHERE
It's most needed
Under-served geographies with little civil-society presence — Eastern UP, Odisha tribal belt, NE India.
The bridge

Seeding Fellowships takes capable young professionals, gives them an 8-month runway to become a founder, then a 2-year seed grant to build. The geographies get lasting civil-society presence; the founders get a real launchpad; the Foundation's expansion plans get talent aligned to its priorities.

OBJECTIVE 1

Support young professionals to set up and run their own organisations.

OBJECTIVE 2

Increase civil-society presence in under-served geographies, working with vulnerable populations.

OBJECTIVE 3

Feed the Foundation's expansion in focus geographies and thematic areas.

The offer to fellows

₹45k/mo for 8 months, then ₹20L pa for 2 years to build.

The fellowship is the runway. The seed grant is the vehicle. Setting up the organisation is non-negotiable.

WHO CAN APPLY
Under 40, on-field, ready to move

Under-40 · 4+ years on-field experience · based in or willing to fully relocate to the geography · individuals or groups of up to 3.

FELLOWSHIP · 8 MONTHS
₹45,000/mo

Stipend for 8 months. Residential orientation, guided exposure, monthly training on finance, compliance, governance, org set-up.

SEED GRANT · 2 YEARS
₹20L pa

Or as per our cost templates for the specific programme. Founder stipend continues at ₹45k/mo. Awarded only on satisfactory completion of fellowship milestones.

The point (non-negotiable)

Setting up the organisation is the point. The fellowship exists to take someone from potential founder to registered, functioning organisation. Fellows must take that path.

The deal, said clearly upfront

Gated: each phase has milestones to proceed. Seed grant releases only after registration + compliance. Continuation across the 2 years is performance-based.

The design fork

Two tracks. Same offer, different degree of prescription.

How prescriptive we are, how much freedom the fellow gets, how closely we monitor after the seed grant is awarded — all vary by track.

TRACK 1 · PRESCRIPTIVE

Scale our existing work

Fellow scales what's already proven — to our guidelines.
  • Specific brief: e.g. set up 10 crèches in a defined Bangalore cluster, to our guidelines, budget and resourcing structure.
  • Limited seats — clearly stated per cluster / programme.
  • We have guidelines, training capacity and existing programme frames.
  • Closer monitoring, aligned to the programme's review cadence.
  • Works where our operational teams are on the ground.
  • Learning postings with existing partners in that domain.
TRACK 2 · OPEN

Seed new organisations

Fellow works up their own programme plan.
  • High design freedom: fellow shapes their own programme with our exposure and guidance.
  • Broader intake within the focused geographies.
  • For themes where need is clear but no implementation protocol exists.
  • Lighter, outcome- and milestone-oriented monitoring post-grant.
  • Example: forest rights for Chenchu communities, community sanitation where no protocol exists.
  • Fellow plans their own exposure + partner interactions.
Common across both tracks

Compliance and org-setup support (registration, 12A/80G, statutory) · residential orientation · capacity-building trainings · foundation-panel proposal review · seed grant on satisfactory completion · quarterly residentials during the 2-year seed grant.

The 8 months

Phases 1–4 (orientation through registration) make up the 8-month fellowship. Our role across these is to expose, train and gate — not to manage. Fellows own their proposals; we set the guardrails.

Who does what

Central designs the frame. Geographies do the work.

Rollout is geography-based and staggered, gated on completion of needs assessment, demand generation and hiring in each geo.

  • Compliance support (via consultant / agency)

Position 1 · leads ≠ applications

Applications only exist after the portal opens. Before that we build reach + captured leads. Reach ≠ lead ≠ application — three separate metrics, counted separately.

Position 2 · digital = ground

Owned handles + WhatsApp broadcast weighted equally with in-person events. Views and follows are reach; only a form submission is a lead.

Position 3 · rolling screening

Screening happens in weekly batches inside the window, not after. Selection completes by end-Dec, saving ~6 weeks vs sequential.

Why rolling screening

Screen weekly, not after window close. Selection completes by end-Dec — ~6 weeks faster than sequential. Notice periods for 4+ yr professionals become the binding constraint, not our process.

CV alumni fast-track

Applicants who are alumni of our own programmes get an accelerated selection path — they don't skip stages, but calibration is quicker and panel time is shorter.

Why informal works

Our own operational team is in that cluster. They provide day-to-day presence, ground truth, and early course-correction. That's the accountability layer — no formal partner structure is needed. This is why track 2 postings work: we're not asking partners to be HR.

The SPOC / coordinator

Single point of contact so the partner is never chased by multiple people. Makes email intros, convenes joint meetings, records milestone evidence in MIS, folds partner conversation into monthly review. Geo POC owns escalation and protects partner goodwill.
