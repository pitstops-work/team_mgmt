Reading the diagram: the ground floor belongs to women, children and water — the anganwadi with its own separate street entrance, the women's toilets and bathing, the unisex accessible WC, and the street-facing water ATM. Men's toilets and bathing move to the first floor beside the laundry and drying deck — the standard multi-storey pattern, with the ground accessible WC covering anyone who can't climb. The second floor restores the Samudhaya Bhavana as a community hall with library and CBO office. Greywater drops to the DEWATS buried in the rear court, whose sealed slab doubles as the anganwadi's fenced play court, and returns as recycled flush water. Dimensions assume the ~12.0 × 12.5 m footprint read from the structural report's sketch; the toposurvey in the DPR fixes the actuals.
