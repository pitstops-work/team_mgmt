Part One

What we do on the ground.

Not a pitch. The everyday work — in the Bangalore settlements most of the city never sees.

The reality we work in

A large share of the city lives in places the city plans around.

Informal settlements hold the workers a city runs on — and the services a city promises rarely reach all the way in. The gap isn't a lack of schemes. It's the last mile.

THE PEOPLE

Construction workers, domestic workers, waste-pickers, street vendors — the informal workforce a city depends on, living in dense settlements.

THE GAP

Early childhood, learning, sanitation, food, health, entitlements — services that exist on paper but thin out at the settlement edge.

THE MISS

Most efforts sample. They touch a few settlements, run a pilot, and move on. Coverage stays shallow and short.

Our starting conviction

You don't change a settlement by visiting it. You change it by staying — showing up in the same places, week after week, across everything a family needs, until the services actually hold. That conviction shapes everything on the next few slides.

Not one programme. A whole system around a family.

A child born in a settlement can grow up inside our care — crèche, then learning centre — while the household gets clean water, sanitation, food security, health, and the entitlements it's owed. Breadth is the point.

Why breadth matters

A single service in isolation leaks — a fed child who can't read, a toilet no one maintains. Running these together, in the same places, is what makes each one hold. That's the difference between a project and a programme.

How we work · the operating model

Saturate, don't sample. Rooted in the community. Relentless in follow-up.

The reason the work holds isn't goodwill — it's rigour, and it's led by people from the settlements themselves. Four things we do differently.

👥
We map with the community
Our teams are from these settlements, and our community collectives help name and understand every one. The picture of the ground is built by the people who live there — with them, never done to them.
🎯
We work cluster by cluster
A neighbourhood at a time, to saturation — not a scatter of pilots. When we're in a cluster, we're in all of it.
📱
We use a tool built for this
Field teams plan their visits and note what's done in a simple tool made for exactly this — so effort is shared, handovers are clean, and nothing important rests on memory alone.
🔁
We follow up, relentlessly
Regular visits, honest review, and intense engagement by people the families already know and trust. Nothing is a one-off — showing up again is the whole point.
What this means in practice

Teams who know their settlements by name, who are of the community they serve, and who keep showing up — week after week — so no family quietly falls through the gaps. That care and consistency on the ground is the foundation everything in Part 2 is built on.

What the numbers really say

Not reach for its own sake — saturation. In the settlements we're in, we don't touch a few families. We stay with nearly all of them, across nearly everything they need. Depth, not a headline.

And yet — only 15%

Five years in, we reach barely 15% of Bangalore's deprived communities. The other 85% aren't waiting for a scheme — they're waiting for someone to do the deep, patient work. There just aren't enough organisations willing to. That gap is why the next part exists.

Our commitment

We don't do drive-by development.

The hardest and most valuable thing in this work is staying. It's also the thing almost no one does.

  • We stay put. We don't run a settlement for a funding cycle and leave. We build to remain — for years, not seasons.
  • We go deep before we go wide. A new place gets our full model, or it waits. We'd rather do fewer places completely than many places thinly.
  • We build institutions, not events. Crèches, centres and systems that outlast any one campaign.
  • We back our people. The teams doing this are trained, equipped and supported to make it their life's work — not a stint.
Why this matters to you

Everything you just saw — the model, the discipline, the staying power — is the platform we're about to open up. In Part 2, the question turns to you: what would you build, if all of this stood behind you?

Part Two · a preview

people who want to build.

Everything from here is early — being shaped, not yet announced. We're sharing it now because we'd rather you help shape it than read about it later.

The idea

Back the right people to build the next wave — as founders.

We've spent years proving what works for Bangalore's urban poor. The bottleneck now isn't the model — it's that too few organisations in the city are willing to do this deep, long-haul work. So we're going to back the people who are — with capital, a runway, and a team already on the ground.

THE SHIFT
From working in the sector to building in it

You've spent years delivering other people's programmes. This is the chance to own one — to be the founder, not the field officer.

THE BET
Proven model + committed builder

A good model in the wrong hands stalls. A great builder with no model burns years. We're pairing the two — deliberately.

THE OUTCOME
A real organisation, run by you

Not a project inside ours. Your own organisation — registered, funded and standing on its own, serving a part of the city you make yours.

The language we mean it in

Think of it the way you'd think of a founding team getting backed to launch — seed capital, a runway, mentors, and a platform — except the return is measured in children in crèches and families with clean water, not in equity.

Why Bangalore is the fastest start

You don't start from a blank page. You start from what already works.

Most people who want to do good in this sector lose years reinventing the basics — the model, the budgets, the training, the trust on the ground. Here, in Bangalore, none of that is yours to figure out alone.

What we hand you

A model that's already proven in this city — crèches, learning centres, sanitation, food. A working playbook. Costed budgets. A trained team beside you. And a defined part of the city to make it real in. You bring the drive; you don't have to invent the foundations.

Why founders love this, not fear it

Freedom to design from scratch sounds romantic until year two, when you're still stuck on registration and budgets. Starting from something proven means you spend your energy where it counts — on the ground, on impact, from month one — and you scale faster because the risky part is already de-risked.

Say it plainly

This is the express lane from "I want to build something that matters" to "I run an organisation that does." You inherit years of hard-won learning, take a model that's working, and make it yours across a growing patch of the city. The proof already exists — you get to multiply it.
