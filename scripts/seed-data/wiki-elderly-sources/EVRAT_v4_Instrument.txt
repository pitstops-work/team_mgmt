Elder Vulnerability Rapid Assessment Tool (EVRAT) v4
The EVRAT is a structured community caregiving assessment tool for identification, categorisation, prioritisation, follow-up planning, and dynamic reassessment of elders living in urban poor settlements and resettlement communities under the Chennai Elderly Care Programme.
The tool follows a progressive layered assessment structure:
	•	Part A — Initial Vulnerability Screening (IVS)
	•	Part B — Expanded Vulnerability Assessment (EVA)
	•	Critical Risk Flag Assessment
	•	Category Assignment
	•	Care Plan & Follow-up (Section E, restructured in v4)
+ NEW SECTION  •  Field markers added in v4
Every field is tagged with one of three markers:
	•	[B] — Baseline: captured once; updated only on life-state change (address, household composition, entitlement obtained, tier reassigned).
	•	[V] — Visit: captured fresh at every assessment, including reassessments. Trajectory is read from these.
	•	[B/V] — Captured at baseline; refreshed at visit if changed.
Reassessment generates a new record — never overwrite previous data. Trajectory is read by comparing visits.
Vishnu Harikumar: Recommendation 3 — Adds baseline/visit markers to every field so COs know which to re-fill at each visit. The change is structural: it converts the tool from a snapshot into a longitudinal instrument without adding any new fields.

Calibration Note
+ NEW SECTION  •  Inter-rater reliability footnote added in v4
This tool requires inter-rater calibration before rollout and quarterly thereafter (see Operational Guidelines Section 19). Without calibration, the same elder may be scored differently by different COs, and trajectory comparisons become noise rather than signal. Calibration is a tool-improvement exercise, not a performance evaluation of COs.
Vishnu Harikumar: Recommendation 15 — Calibration footnote added. Full protocol in Guidelines Section 19.
